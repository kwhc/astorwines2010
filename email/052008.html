our top 11:
Northern Italian Wines
(... we go to eleven)

11. Müller-Thurgau "Eisacktaler," St. Magdalena (2007)
A cross of Riesling and Silvaner, the white Müller-Thurgau grape is more typically found in Germany, but does extremely well in the high altitudes of the Alto Adige in Northern Italy. This one is a delicious, fruit-forward, dry white wine with grapefruit and apple aromas.
#11264
Sale Price: $16.99

10. Bianchetta Ü Pastine, Bisson (2006)
A dry white wine from the rare Bianchetta grape variety, this absolutely bursts with vibrant white peach flavors and firm mineral character. Born near the sea, it naturally pairs well with foods from the sea.
#10733
Sale Price: $14.44

9. Ribolla Gialla, Dorigo (2006)
A white wine from the fascinating wine region of Friuli, made from the Ribolla Gialla grape. Floral and exotic, with good acidity to liven the senses, this is delicious with seafood, chicken, and other delicate dishes.
#10850
Sale Price: $15.29

8. Moscato Giallo, Caldaro (2006)
An off-dry white made from the Moscato Giallo grape. Spicy and floral aromas on the palate with great acidity and finesse. A great apéritif wine that will pair well with young, soft cheeses. Fresh, fruity, and easy-drinking. Give this just a light chill.
#10190
Sale Price: $11.89

7. Spigau Crociata "Terredamare," Le Rocche del Gatto (2004)
Fear not this bold, earthy, dry white wine that is near amber in color. Made from Pigato, it has precise floral aromas, fresh pear fruits, and great acidity. Drink it cool, but don't overchill.
#16516
Sale Price: $15.29

6. Diana, Trevisani (2003)
This Northern Italian red comes from vines planted near the stunning Lake Garda. Based on the rare Rebo grape variety, the wine is fleshy in texture, with loads of juicy dark fruit character and a slight mineral edge on the finish.
#17594
Sale Price: $12.74

5. Teroldego Rotaliano, De Vescovi-Ulzbach (2006)
If you're a fan of California Syrah or Zinfandel, try a red Teroldego from Italy. The grapes for this exceptional example come from old vines planted in limestone-based soil, high up in the Dolomites. Dark in color, the wine displays concentrated aromas of juicy dark currants, violets, graphite, and stony minerals on the finish. Hand-harvested and fermented in steel tanks with naturally occuring vineyard yeasts.
#16238
Sale Price: $16.14

4. Schioppettino, Girolamo Dorigo (2006)
What's this? Why, Schioppettino, of course. One of the many great grapes indigenous to Friuli, Schiopettino makes medium-bodied red wines with floral notes and rustic acidity. Pair this with gamey foods or hard cheeses..
#15638
Sale Price: $18.69

3. Valpolicella, Le Ragose (2004)
A deeply colored wine of voluptuous aromatics, such as freshly macerated black cherries, violets, and red peppercorns. With focused fruit and mouthwatering acidity, this is a perfect accompaniment to your next rich meal.
#11642
Sale Price: $16.99

2. Amarone, Trabucchi (2003)
This staff favorite hails from the eastern part of the Valpolicella sub-region. Made from some well-loved grapes indigenous to the area (Corvina, Corvinone, Molinara, Rondinella) as well as some not-so-common ones (Croatina, Dindarella, Forsellina, Oseleta, and Rossignola), this is a dry, voluptuous, concentrated red wine with flavors of dark berries and currants.
#16915
Sale Price: $38.24

1. SLagrein Riserva, Mayr-Nusser (2004)
Coming from one of our favorite small producers, this is full of dark berries, leather, smoke, and earth. This wine has exuberant dark-berried fruit character, an elegant texture, and a note of powder on the finish.
#12930
Sale Price: $24.64
