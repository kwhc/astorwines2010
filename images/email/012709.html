7. Duckhorn Merlot - 2006 (750 mL)
Red fruits, dark fruits, baking spices, and soft texture. This is one of those immediately appealing wines that keeps revealing endless layers of complexity - especially with some time in the glass. Absolutely brilliant.

6. Estancia Merlot, Central Coast - 2004 (750 mL)
Big, dark, chocolatey fruit flavors characterize this wine from Estancia, one of California's most consistent wineries. This shows that unmistakable Merlot mouthfeel, followed by a long, oak-kissed finish.

5. Sonoma Vineyards Merlot - 2003 (750 mL)

4. Bogle Merlot - 2006 (750 mL)
Bogle’s Merlot is another great example of the soft and silky wine that this varietal can be. Concentrated aromas of earthy spice and cherries surround the senses. Mere hints of pipe tobacco and spicy oak lend depth to the finish, reinforced by firm tannins.
#99825

Sale Price: $7.99

3. Castle Rock Napa Valley Merlot - 2005 (750 mL)
A generous wine with ripe, full-bodied fruit, soft tannins, and good length. This has all the velvety texture of a Pomerol: try it if you've been unsure about entering the realm of Merlot lately.

Sale Price: $8.49

Aged in small oak barrels, this Merlot is the essence of approachable, satisfying red wine. The oak imparts subtle spicy vanilla notes on the nose, while the palate is kept happy with plenty of dark fruit flavors. This goes with almost anything.
