Our Top 11
Burgundies
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Bourgogne Blanc, La Burgette - 2007
This Chardonnay is dry yet fruity, fascinating on its own yet lovely with food, and full of plush yellow fruit flavors like plum, Meyer lemon, and Golden Delicious apples. Actually, “Golden Delicious” would be a perfect nickname for this wine.
#18415
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

10. St. Aubin Rouge 1er Cru "Les Rouges Gorges", Prudhon - 2005
This 1er Cru bottling is made from fruit sourced from three lieu-dits, giving it charm and complexity. The Prudhon estate makes exceedingly feminine wines; this one combines earthy cassis fruit, wild berries, and spice.
#21316
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

9. Bourgogne Hautes-Côtes de Beaune Rouge, Montchovet - 2005
A lovely, soft, ripe-fruited Burgundy that is ready to serve tonight. Montchovet does a slow fermentation in barrel, followed by a year of barrel age. Still, the wine is far from oaky and primarily shows plush charm.
#21310
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

8. Meursault "Perrières", Bitouzet-Prieur - 2003
A complex white wine from a winemaker who is a staunch traditionalist in the cellar. This is full of raw honey, yellow apples, and quince fruit on the palate. The warm conditions of 2003 made this Meursault a bit fuller-bodied than most.
#19295
Original Price: $64.99
Sale Price: $55.24

7. Monthélie, Jean Garaudet - 2006
The village of Monthélie is primarily known for the brilliant red wines it produces. This Pinot Noir is no exception: vibrantly colored and packed with red berry fruit (just as the winemaker intended), it has enormous immediate appeal.
#21275
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

6. Pouilly-Fuissé, Léonard de St. Aubin - 2004
Pouilly-Fuissé is the name of a village located just north of the Beaujolais region, as well as a white wine made there from Chardonnay grapes. This one shows nicely rounded notes of tangerine and honeysuckle, with a long, mineral-laden finish.
#19936
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

5. Morgon, Lapierre - 2007
Lapierre is a fanatical grape-grower, and we're very glad to be able to reap the benefits. This is amazing for the beauty of its fruit and the purity of its flavor.
#11341
Original Price: $21.97
Sale Price: $18.67

4. Viré-Clessé, Dom. de Roally - 2005
Chardonnay from the Mâcon is hardly ever made from estate-grown grapes. Here's a rich version that's full and round in the mouth with yellow apple fruit and fresh almonds on the finish.
#12727
Original Price: $26.99
Sale Price: $22.94

3. "Allez Goûtons", Vin de Table, Derain - 2007
Aligoté is known for its bracing acidity and minerality, and this bottle is no exception. The nose is herbal and citrusy with hints of green apple fruit. Make sure to enjoy this with seafood: they're perfect partners.
#21307
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

2. Ladoix, Domaine Petitot - 2005
The 2005 Burgundy vintage (along with talented growers, of course) produced ripe, sweet-fruited wines from less prestigious villages, at prices that normal humans could afford. This red wine is full of licorice, red fruit, and earth. Decant for best results.
#21135
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

1. Pommard "Noizons", Jean Garaudet - 2006
Vines here, which range in age from 20-80 years, grow in terraced vineyards at 250m above sea level, and the soil is an iron-rich clayey chalk. Superb concentration combined with great elegance for the appellation.
#19737
Original Price: $48.99
Sale Price: $41.64
