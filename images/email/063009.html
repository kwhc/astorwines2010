Astor's Top Portuguese Wines
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Santa Sara, J.P. Vinhos - 2007
Santa Sara is part of the new breed of juicy, friendly wines from the Alentejo region, and it's quite a popular bottle around here. Made from Tempranillo and indigenous Portuguese red grapes, it is packed with bright fruit and sturdy tannins.
#16536
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

10. Vinho Tinto Douro, Lavradores de Feitoria - 2006
This red wine comes from a collection of growers dedicated to making some fantastic dry red wines in the Douro Valley. This is plush with fruit, dark berries and all-around approachability. A red wine to pair with any rich meal, whether it be burgers or a roast.
#19700
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

9. Periquita, Fonseca - 2005
Periquita (meaning "parakeet") is a flavorful red wine bearing the name of the most widely grown grape in the vast Terras do Sado region. A formidable structure makes this wine great with grilled meats or aged cheeses.
#52334
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

8. "FP" Filipa Pato "Ensaios", Baga - 2007
This wine is made by Filipa Pato, daughter of Portugal's Luis Pato. This is a rich red wine made from a blend of the local grapes Touriga Nacional, Baga, and Alfrocheira. The grapes are vinified in steel tanks before going into barrel. There's a distinct smokiness on the palate with a lot of dark berries, blueberries, and espresso foam on the finish.
#18455
Original Price: $20.99
Sale Price: $17.84

7. Vinho Verde, Quinta Santa Maria - 2007
A crisp and dry style of Vinho Verde that is richer and more complex than the average. Perfect with seafood.
#21407
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

6. Cardeal Reserva, Touriga Nacional, Dão - 2002
Made from Touriga Nacional, this fresh red wine shows firm dark fruit, warm spices, and a gentle touch of fresh tobacco on the finish. It has a great tannic structure that allows it to hold up to big, meaty dishes.
#13263
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

5. Santa Fé de Arraiolos, Vinho Regional Alentejano - 2006
An excellent red if you're looking for something dark, rich, and dripping with black fruit flavors. This is a fresh and lightly tannic wine, and it shines with a huge range of meat-based cuisines.
#19451
Original Price: $7.99
Sale Price: $6.79

4. Vinho Verde, "Gazela", Sogrape - 2008
Gazela is a light, crisp and fruity Vinho Verde from the Minho region in northwest Portugal. With a slight sparkle on the tongue, this seafood-friendly wine has attractive lemon-apricot aromas. Its gentle fruitiness is balanced by bright acidity.
#52424
Original Price: $4.99
Sale Price: $4.24

3. Álvaro Castro Tinto, Dão - 2007
This is an intensely fragrant, full-bodied red wine from the Dão region of Portugal. Alvaro Castro is a meticulous winemaker whose overarching goal is to ensure that varietal typicity shows in his finished wines. This bottle is full of dark, compote-like black fruits that are accessible in spite of their intensity. Of course, this would be a great match for grilled skirt steak and the like.
#18456
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

2. Vinho de Mesa Rosé, Famega - NV
This is a deliciously refreshing rosé from Portugal. Notes of citrus, strawberry, and a dusting of white pepper make this perfect for sipping over a long, leisurely evening.
#18172
Original Price: $5.99
Sale Price: $5.09

1. Quinta dos Aciprestes, Douro - 2005
From the steep hillsides of the Douro River valley, where the great fortified Ports are born, comes this lush, silky, dry red wine. Made up of a blend of deeply colored Port grapes, its rich plum flavors and black pepper notes are ideal with beef or lamb.
#51024
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19
