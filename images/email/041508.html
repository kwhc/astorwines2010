our top 11:
Rhône Valley Wines
(... we go to eleven)

11. Côtes du Rhône "Grandes Vignes", D'Estezargues (2006)
This great wine from a Natural winemaker is full of dark fruit and a little bit smoky, with just a hint of game. Great now in the flush of youth but also has interesting medium-term aging potential.
#26323
Sale Price: $10.19

10. Crozes-Hermitage "La Matinière", Ferraton (2004)
Made entirely from Syrah grown in sand and limestone soils. The sand helps make for a soft and fruity Northern Rhône wine. The fruit is abundant but soft in its approach. This is drinking very well today.
#16909
Sale Price: $15.29

9. Séguret Côtes du Rhône, Sélection Laurence Féraud (2005)
Coming from one of the newer declared villages of the Rhône valley, this is a delicious Côtes du Rhône. Earthy notes, with bright red fruit and juicy acidity.
#19385
Sale Price: $16.99

8. Côtes du Rhône, Ch. Du Mourre de Tendre (2003)
This is traditional Côtes du Rhône at its best.  Made from old-vine Grenache, Cinsault, and Carignan, this wine is aged for two years before being released and does not see any filtration.  Rich and concentrated, with notes of plum, blackberry, and mocha.
#19595
Sale Price: $11.04

7. Dom. de Fenouillet Rosé, Côtes du Ventoux (2007)
A very pretty pink with a coppery tint, this wine bursts with melon flavors, stones, and just a touch of pepper spice.
#11887
Sale Price: $8.49

6. Dom. Triennes Viognier, Ste. Fleur (2006)
A fresh, dry white wine with apricot and peach floral aromas and lovely minerals on the finish. This is a fruit-laden Viognier that you can enjoy tonight.
#15479
Sale Price: $12.74

5. Côte-Rôtie, "Cuvée Classique", Rostaing (2004)
This entry-level cuvée comes from René Rostaing, one of the masters of the Northern Rhône. The Cuvée Classique is a blend of grapes from different vineyards in the Côte-Rôtie. Dark blueberry, tobacco, and brambly tannins characterize this wine.
#16330
Sale Price: $42.49

4. Crozes-Hermitage, "Les Jalets," Jaboulet (2005)
Soft and rich with spicy blackcurrant flavor, this value-driven Rhône red is made by noted producer Paul Jaboulet-Aîné. Some of the Syrah is sourced from younger vines that are grown in the same soil as the superb Thalabert Crozes-Hermitage.
#24484
Sale Price: $16.99

3. Ermitage "Les Dionnières", Ferraton (1999)
One of those tight and brooding reds that's made for the cellar. This one is just beginning to evolve. Full of leather, spicebox, blackcurrants and limey minerals. The tannins are elegant and silken.
#16910
Sale Price: $52.69

2. Côtes du Rhône "Cuvée de la Pierre Chaud", L'Anglore (2006)
Made from Grenache by natural winemaker Eric Pfifferling. A prominent floral nose leads to freshly macerated mixed berries on the palate. This wine is made without the addition of sulphur, allowing it to retain a rare purity and cleanness of flavor. Delicious.
#14099
Sale Price: $16.99

1. Syrah, Hervé Souhaut (2006)
Hervé Souhaut makes wine west of the St.-Joseph sub-region of the Northern Rhône Valley. He uses the Syrah grape, striving at every turn to preserve its natural fruit character. Dark berried fruits on the palate lead to violets and eucalyptus on the finish. This is a fresh, unadulterated, and profoundly complex red wine.
#14105
Sale Price: $21.24
