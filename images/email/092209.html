Astor's Top 11 Wines from Piedmont
...Our lists go up to 11.


11. Gavi di Gavi, La Merlina - 2007
Another great white wine from Piedmont. Dry and a touch reticent upon opening, this flourishes after a few minutes in the glass with notes of raw honey, fresh lemons, and rusty minerality.
#12842
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

10. Grignolino "Poggeto", La Casaccia - 2008
This tasty red wine from Piedmont is made from the Grignolino grape, and comes from 50-year-old vines. The pale cherry-red color is captivating. The palate boasts the freshest imaginable red berry flavors, with hints of white peppercorn. This red has such pristine fruit and great mineral-laced acidity that you'll be aching for more before you know it.
#12753
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

9. Verduno Pelaverga, Fratelli Alessandria - 2007
A medium-bodied red wine from the northern Piedmontese area of Verduno. The rare Pelaverga grape is grown mostly in this region, where chalky soils predominate - and that terroir clearly shines through in the wine. Aromas of red berries, black tea, and that chalkiness work very well together here.
#13970
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

8. Freisa d'Asti "Cento Filari", Lo Spaventapasseri - 2006
The name of this estate means "little sparrow" in Italian. Freisa can sometimes be a touch pétillant, but not in this case: this red wine is completely without fizz. With classic notes of Piedmontese soil on the nose, dark fruits melding with purple flowers, and hints of tar, this is a light-bodied and lively wine.
#21931
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.99

7. Ghemme, Cantalupo - 2004
Ghemme is an appellation in Piedmont known for its lighter, more approachable Nebbiolo. Violets, rose petals, earthy cherries, and fine rustic acidity shine through in this distinctively northern Italian red wine.
#41007
Original Price: $35.99
Sale Price: $30.59

6. Nebbiolo d'Alba, Le Formiche - 2007
This rich, ripe, dark-fruited Langhe Rosso is made from 100% Nebbiolo. On the nose you'll get earthy, tarry notes as well as blackberry and plum. The fruit flavors are quite ripe and sweet but with a spicy, tannic edge and a hint of espresso bean on the finish. Drink with hearty fall fare.
#21999
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

5. Nebbiolo d'Alba "Valmaggione", Brovia - 2005
A great value in Nebbiolo for those of you who crave Barolo but are saving that bottle for the right occasion. This is drinking very well right now: berries, plums, and almonds shine through on the palate, and the finish is firm and tannic.
#40019
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $28.99

4. Barbera d'Alba, De Forville - 2007
A pulpy, plump Barbera wine that is vinified in steel and then aged in large oak barrels for nearly ten months. Dry and deliciously fruity, with bold flavors of dark berries and plums.
#21989
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $12.99

3. Langhe Rosso "Superboum", Az. Agr. Bricco del Cucu - 2007
This Langhe Rosso is a blend of Dolcetto and Merlot. The wine is fermented and aged in stainless steel to preserve the wine's enticing dark cherry aromas and flavors. On the label, you'll see a depiction of a local fresco. The name of the wine is a play on words, meaning both "pride" in Latin and "supercool" in contemporary Italian slang. This is a fabulous bottle, full of concentrated, sweet fruit but also structure and tannin, resulting in a superbly balanced finished product.
#21988
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $14.99

2. Barbaresco "Boito," Rizzi - 2005
Rizzi's Grand Cru vineyard "Boito" consists mostly of old Nebbiolo vines planted on chalk-based soils. The wine shows great structure, full of violets and rose petals on the palate - the quintessential attributes of traditionally crafted Barbaresco.
#16268
Original Price: $42.99
Sale Price: $36.54

1. Barolo, Brovia - 2004
A red wine blended from younger vines found within the four crus of the Brovia estate. This has smooth tannins on the palate, with a real woodsy/spicy character and red fruits. Always a compelling Nebbiolo from one of our favorite estates in Piedmont.
#13672
Original Price: $49.99
Sale Price: $42.49
