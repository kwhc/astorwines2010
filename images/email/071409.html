Astor's Top Austrian Wines
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Grüner Veltliner "Rabenstein", Dürnberg Falkensteiner Rieden - 2007 (750)
A complex and yellow-fruited Grüner that shows lovely round character in the mouth. This is definitely a full and plush rendition of the grape that will delight you if you're not a fan of its more herbal and citric renditions.
#21509
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $14.99

10. Zweigelt Rosé, Nigl - 2008 (750)
Imagine a Loire Valley rosé but full of even more exuberant raspberry, cranberry, and tart cherry fruit on the nose. Though the aromatics reminded us of the Loire, we were delighted to find that the wine wasn't as astringent as some Loire examples can be. Refreshing and delicious.
#21627
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

9. Grüner Veltliner, Glatzer - 2007 (750)
This basic Grüner from Glatzer will knock your socks off. It is approachable and full of fresh, zingy, herbal notes, as well as peppery freshness on the nose and palate. Perfect if you have yet to experience the magic of Grüner.
#21641
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

8. Grüner Veltliner "Obere Steigen", Huber - 2007 (750)
Grüner Veltliner comes in many forms, and this is one of its rounder and more full-fruited manifestations. The wine is unoaked and dry, with a broad mid-palate full of white flowers and pears.
#20465
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

7. Blauer Zweigelt, Berger - 2007 (LTR)
A cross between the Blaufränkisch and St.-Laurent grapes, the richly colored Blauer Zweigelt grape was developed in 1922 – by one Dr. Zweigelt. This value-driven Austrian red wine is round and smooth, with lively cherry scents and flavors.
#56674
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

6. Riesling Dürnstein Federspiel, Alzinger - 2007 (750)
A dry Riesling packed with lime and kumquat fruit, notes of petrol and lots of minerals. Despite its power, this magnificent wine maintains a perfect balance among all its elements.
#19215
Original Price: $36.99
Sale Price: $31.44

5. Temento, Tement - 2008 (750)
This light, aromatic white wine is a blend of Welschriesling and Sauvignon Blanc from the limestone hills of Austria. Welschriesling (no relation to Riesling) adds beautiful citrus and floral notes to this wine as a hint of clove spice peeks through. Perfect as an apéritif, or paired with green salads and asparagus.
#12615
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

4. Riesling "Gobelsburger", Schloss Gobelsburg - 2008 (750)
With notes of green tea, verbena, pear, lime-blossom, and wisteria, this is an absolute masterpiece from the Schloss Gobelsburg estate. A gorgeous, structured, and focused Riesling.
#15553
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. Blaufränkisch, Neckenmarkter, Moric - 2006 (750)
With this wine, it is truly a challenge to decide which is more amazing, the nose or the palate. The nose gives intense dark berry and floral, violet notes. The palate is focused with well-integrated tannins that indicate great potential to age. Fortunately the only decision one needs to make is the decision to take a bottle home. The wine is aged for 18-20 months in oak but woodiness doesn't show on the nose or palate. Cellar or decant for best results.
#20780
Original Price: $52.99
Sale Price: $45.04

2. St. Laurent "Klassisch", Rosi Schuster - 2007 (750)
A complex Austrian red showing peppercorn, cumin, and dark berry notes on the nose. St. Laurent is a relative of Pinot Noir, so expect a lighter style of wine marked by the great acidity that is a strength of cool-climate wines. Delicious, ripe, and unwooded.
#21358
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

1. Grüner Veltliner, Berger - 2008 (LTR)
Generally, though this is our oldest liter bottle of Grüner, it is also the best. It's incredibly zingy, with lemon and apple acidity, but also ripe and inviting... almost dangerously so. A perfect choice for parties, and for everyday drinking with an assortment of lighter foods.
#20808
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04
