Astor's Top Rhône Wines
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Côtes-du-Rhône Rosé, Domaine de la Bastide - 2008
Despite its "watermelon Jolly Rancher" color, this rosé is bone-dry, with intense cherry notes on the nose and crystalline fruits on the palate.
#21349
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

10. Viognier VdP, Cuilleron - 2007
Born in the hills of the northern Rhône, this Viognier is rich with notes of peaches and spice. Winemaker Yves Cuilleron is a fourth-generation vigneron who strives to revitalize Seyssuel's vineyard, which was popular in Roman times.
#23841
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

9. Gigondas, Dom. de Cabasse - 2005
Here is a spicy and minerally Gigondas that will benefit from a few years in the cellar (or you can simply decant it before dinner). Dark fruits abound on the palate, and this shows lots of structure, tang, and tannin for a Grenache-based Rhône wine.
#20933
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

8. Côtes du Rhône "Cuvée la Vignerone", Gour de Chaulé - 2007
Aline Bonfils has created an uncommonly complex Grenache-based red wine. Old vines impart concentration and depth, while aromas of undergrowth nicely set off the approachable dark fruit. Great with grilled steak.
#15669
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

7. Côte-Rôtie, "Cuvée Classique", Rostaing - 2004
This entry-level cuvée comes from René Rostaing, one of the masters of the northern Rhône. The Cuvée Classique is a blend of grapes from different vineyards in the Côte-Rôtie, and it shows dark blueberry, tobacco, and brambly tannins.
#16330
Original Price: $49.99
Sale Price: $42.49

6. Crozes-Hermitage, Dom. Mucyn - 2007
The Crozes-Hermitage appellation is located in the northern part of the Rhône Valley. At the Mucyn estate there's an abundance of granite on their vine slopes resulting is a rich, full-bodied red wine that isn't affected by new oak. The emphasis here is on the Syrah fruit: spice, smoke, dark berries, and licorice. Full of complexities that evolve in the glass.
#20675
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

5. Counoise VdP, Ramière, Monpertuis - 2006
A very pretty Vin de Pays from the southern Rhône. It's on the lighter side, a little bit rustic, fairly distinctive, and very charming and delicious.
#46521
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

4. Vacqueyras Blanc, Dom. de Montvac - 2006
Vacqueyras Blanc is a rare find. Made from Roussanne, Clairette and Bourboulenc, this white wine shows a rich, creamy mouthfeel and underlying toasty notes. Domaine de Montvac has been handed down from mother to daughter for 4 generations. These vines are 30 years old and grown in chalky clay soils, giving this wine an alluring minerality.
#18440
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

3. St.-Joseph, Graillot - 2007
Alain Graillot, one of the top producers in the northern Rhone appellation of Crozes-Hermitage, also produces a minuscule amount of Saint-Joseph from a 1 ½-acre plot of 20-year-old Syrah vines planted in decomposed granite soils. Fermentation takes place in concrete, and the wine is aged for 12 months in 1- to 6-year-old barrels. This has the explosive, vivid, deep, and peppery fruit that characterizes Alain’s wines, but with the unique charm and freshness of a Saint-Joseph.
#10146
Original Price: $37.99
Sale Price: $32.29

2. VdT "Cuvée du Sommelier", Dom. de Boissan - NV
A light and extremely tasty young-vine Grenache that shows the skill of vigneron Christian Bonfils. Juicy and showing lots of tart cherry and sumac on the nose, this is sure to delight on its own as a summer quaffer or with lighter fare.
#21711
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

1. Tavel, Ch. Trinquevedel - 2008
Here is a dry, candied, and powdery rosé that shows uncanny freshness and vivid acidity on the palate. A blend of Grenache, Bourboulenc, and Clairette, this full-bodied Tavel can be enjoyed all year round, but it adds an especially elegant touch to summer barbecues.
#21621
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44
