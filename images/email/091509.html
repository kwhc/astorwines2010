Astor's Top Burgundies
...Our lists go to 11. And this one includes Beaujolais!


11. Beaujolais-Villages, Granger - 2007
Fresh off the boat and drinking well today. Clean and fruit-driven and very distinctly Beaujolais with raspberry fruit aromas. Shows a fresh acidity that works so well with meat dishes.
#13601
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $12.99

10. Fixin "Les Crais de Chêne", Dom. Collotte - 2007
The village of Fixin is located in the Côte de Nuits. This Pinot Noir is accessible and showing plenty of appealing, youthful red-berried fruit character.
#19952
Original Price: $32.99
Sale Price: $28.04

9. Bourgogne Grande Ordinaire, Montchovet - 2008
This is Gamay-based Bourgogne from 50-year-old vines. Phillip the Bold couldn't possibly have gotten rid of all Burgundy Gamay in the 14th century. Expressive, with lots of wild raspberry on the nose and rustic, minerally tannins on the finish.
#21309
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.99

8. Pommard "Noizons", Jean Garaudet - 2006
Vines here, which range in age from 20 to 80 years, grow in terraced vineyards at 250 meters above sea level, and the soil is an iron-rich clayey chalk. This red shows superb concentration, combined with great elegance for the appellation.
#19737
Original Price: $48.99
Sale Price: $41.64

7. St.-Romain Blanc "Les Perrières", Buisson - 2006
A light-handed style of white Burgundy with emphasis on the pear and apple flavors of Chardonnay. This wine shows earth and mineral notes on the nose and palate, making it a great choice with lots of different dishes. Balanced and succulent.
#21439
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $27.19

6. Chassagne-Montrachet Blanc, "La Bergerie", Jean-Marc Morey - 2005
This is Jean-Marc Morey's basic village Chassagne-Montrachet. This Chardonnay is full of white apple fruit character with raw honey and mineral on the finish. Excellent finesse from a traditional producer whose wines thrive with years of age.
#11736
Original Price: $47.99
Sale Price: $40.79

5. Chablis, Fréderic Gueguen - 2006
Fréderic Gueguen is a passionate winemaker who practices Biodynamics in his vineyards. This is a classic Chablis with steely minerals, good weight and ripe citrus and yellow orchard fruits on the palate.
#22046
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

4. St. Aubin Rouge "Frionnes" 1er Cru, Prudhon - 2006
You can't go wrong with this red beauty from the little-known village of St. Aubin. Prudhon offers value in Pinot Noir. This vintage shows dark cherry fruit, spice and a note of raspberry on the finish.
#12631
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $24.99

3. Morey-St.-Denis "Clos Solon" VV, Fourrier - 2006
From a very old-school Burgundy producer who is rigorous in his pursuit of excellence. A wine that will do very well in the cellar and is best kept there for several years at least.
#13202
Original Price: $53.99
Sale Price: $45.89

2. Bourgogne Rouge "Bons Batons", G. Barthod - 2006
It's no wonder that Ghislaine Barthod's wines have so much finesse, she learned from her father, Gaston, who came from the Jura, where light-handed elegance is the order of the day. Simply the most refined Bourgogne ever.
#21362
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $29.99

1. Pernand-Vergelesses Blanc, Rollin - 2004
Some of the most astounding white Burgundy these days comes from lesser-known villages such as this Chardonnay from Pernand-Vergelesses. You can find tremendous satisfaction in the bouquet itself: cream, leesy floral aromas and mineral. The palate is equally satisfying and clearly points in the direction that this can be held for years to come.
#15289
Original Price: $32.99
Sale Price: $29.99
