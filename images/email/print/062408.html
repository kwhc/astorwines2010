our top 11:
SW France & Midi Wines
(... we go to eleven)

11. VdP Côtes de Thau Rosé, Dom. Gaujal de St. Bon (2007)
Fresh, dry, and approachable, this rosé is made by a talented mother-daughter team. You'll love their style; travel to the south of France and you'll find the locals drinking rosés just like this one.
#13518
Sale Price: $7.64

10. Picpoul de Pinet "Gaujal de St. Bon," Gaujalo (2006)
An exciting and unusual bottling made from the Picpoul Blanc grape in southwest France. Light and dry, this has lemony aromas, raw honey flavors on the palate, and even a nice little kick of mineral on the finish. The Mediterranean climate of this appellation gives its wines plenty of acidity, and this one goes well with a huge range of foods. Intriguing on its own, too.
#12378
Sale Price: $8.49

9. Minervois, Ch. d'Oupia (2006)
An aromatic and densely colored red wine, full-bodied with generous flavors of blackberry and raspberry. Fine tannins, along with accents of pepper and spice, make this an elegant and well-balanced wine.
#26394
Sale Price: $9.34

8. VdP Merlot, Dom. de la Patience (2005)
Here we have a stellar example of high-quality Vin de Pays from a great producer. This fruit-forward Merlot from the Languedoc region shows assertive black cherry flavors and a smooth, velvety mouthfeel. It is made without the use of sulfites by a Natural winemaker.
#15351
Sale Price: $10.19

7. Côtes de Duras, Ch. Haut Lavigne (2007)
This light, refreshing white wine, a blend of Sauvignon Blanc and Sémillon, comes from the tiny AOC of Côtes de Duras, located directly northeast of Bordeaux. Quince notes, a honeyed nose, and a leesy, mineral-driven palate make this the perfect apéritif wine.
#17559
Sale Price: $10.19

6. Corbières "L'Enclos," Dom. des Deux Ânes (2007)
Another great endeavor from this husband-and-wife team in the Corbières area of Languedoc. Aged both in tank and in old barrels, this structured red wine comes from a warm vintage, giving it powerful flavors of black fruit, nutmeg, and dried fig.
#13084
Sale Price: $15.29

5. Jurançon Sec Ch. Jolys (2005)
This light, dry white comes from Jurançon, 30 miles from the Spanish border. With notes of ripe melon, a slight nuttiness, and great acidity, this is the perfect choice with rich shellfish dishes.
#22664
Sale Price: $11.04

4. Le Rosé des Deux Ânes, Vin de Table (2007)
A voluptuous, dry rosé in a luminous magenta hue. This is from the Corbières region of the Languedoc and is made from a blend of the local grapes: mostly Syrah, Carignan, and Grenache. Full of black and red berry fruits with fresh, bright aromas.
#20234
Sale Price: $11.89

3. Carignan VdP de Vallée du Paradis, Clos des Camuzeilles (2004)
rom the Fitou region of the Languedoc, this exceptional wine is made entirely from 70-year-old Carignan vines. Smoky notes on the nose lead to deep plum flavors on the palate.
#13080
Sale Price: $18.69

2. Muscat Sec, Mas las Cabes, Dom. Gardies (2006)
The Muscat grape is usually vinified in a sweet style, but Languedoc winemakers excel at making dry versions like this one. Put just a light chill on this bottle and appreciate all it has to offer: we especially liked its delicate aromas of white flowers, fresh limes, and white pepper.
#12663
Sale Price: $11.04

1. Mas de Daumas Gassac VdP Rouge (2005)
From the top estate from the Languedoc, this red is based on Cabernet Sauvignon with an assortment of indigenous red grapes blended in. Subtle aromas of bay leaf, mixed fresh berries, and violets lead to a finish that shows a pleasantly chalky mineral quality. Delicious and absolutely age-worthy, this is at its best when paired with rich foods, especially roasts.
#14015
Sale Price: $27.19
