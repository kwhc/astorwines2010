Our Top 11
Wines of the Rhône
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Vacqueyras, Le Mas de Collines - 2005 (750 mL)
Le Mas de Collines is a traditional producer in the Rhône. This Vacqueyras is a great example of this. With a medium body and flavors of dark plum and black cherry, this is a delicious food wine, pairing well with everything from charcuterie and cheeses to roast meat.
#15157
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

10. Châteauneuf-du-Pape "Cuvée Classique", Monpertuis - 2005 (750 mL)
This bottle of "Cuvée Classique" comes from a 10-hectare plot. Based on Grenache, Mourvèdre, and Syrah, it gets remarkable concentration and balance from 40-year-old vines.
#18617
Original Price: $40.99
Sale Price: $34.84

9. Viognier VdP, Cuilleron - 2007
Born in the hills of the northern Rhône, this Viognier is rich with notes of peaches and spice. Winemaker Yves Cuilleron is a fourth-generation vigneron who strives to revitalize Seyssuel's vineyard, which was popular in Roman times.
#23841
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

8. Châteauneuf-du-Pape VV, Dom. de la Millière - 2006 (750 mL)
This full-bodied red wine comes from the southern Rhône, and it happens to be a superlative value in traditional Châteauneuf-du-Pape. Full of dark plum flavors and black pepper spice with an unbelievable velvet texture. Winemaker Michel Arnaud is of the 5th generation of Arnauds to work on the domaine.
#18895
Original Price: $35.99
Sale Price: $30.59

7. Counoise VdP, Ramière, Monpertuis - 2006 (750 mL)
A very pretty Vin de Pays from the southern Rhône. It's on the lighter side, a little bit rustic, fairly distinctive, and very charming and delicious.
#46521
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

6. Côtes-du-Rhône, Dom. les Genestas - 2006 (750 mL)
A new discovery from the Rhône. A nearly black ruby color, combined with rich blackberry and black olive notes on the nose, pave the way for the mouth-filling, Syrah-dominated palate. A perfectly full-bodied match for richly flavored meats or cheeses.
#50190
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

5. Tavel "Roc-Epine," Dom. Lafond - 2007 (750 mL)
Tavel is a tiny rosé appellation in the southern Rhône Valley. This dry bottling is like a mixed bowl of cherries on the palate, with a subtle note of watermelon and refreshing acidity that make it go well with lots of foods.
#00058
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

4. Côte-Rôtie Cuvée Classique, R. Rostaing - 2001 (750 mL)
Pure Syrah. The wine is matured in oak barriques with a very conservative use of new oak - no more than 10%. After 18 months' aging this hearty red was fined and bottled without filtration, rendering a more complex wine, made for the ages.
#14758
Original Price: $59.99
Sale Price: $50.99

3. 40ème Rugissants, Estezargues - 2006 (750 mL)
From the Estezargues growers of the Rhône Valley. This is Syrah-based, so it's full of luscious dark berries and pepper. Give this a whirl in a decanter and watch it open up.
#17049
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

2. Châteauneuf du Pape Blanc, Dom. De Nalys - 2006 (750 mL)
White Châteauneuf is extremely rare, and rich yet balanced examples such as this one are even rarer. Full of peach and white flower with a pleasing viscosity that is the result of partial barrel fermentation.
#19746
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $27.19

1. Côtes-du-Rhône, Dom. le Garrigon - 2007 (750 mL)
This is a fantastic, purely fruit-driven wine with supple black cherries and plum fruit riding atop notes of lavender, spice, and mineral. The tannins are subtle but present, making this ideal with meat-based fare.
#18402
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74
