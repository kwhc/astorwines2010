our top 11:
Wines of New Zealand
(... we go to eleven)

11. Martinborough Vineyards Sauvignon Blanc (2007)
This is simply not what springs to mind when we think of New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc! Its finty aromas are reminiscent of Pouilly-Fuissé; it shows beautiful Meyer lemon ripeness on the mid-palate; and salinity shines through on the finish.
#20453
Original Price: $26.99
Sale Price: $22.94

10. Gravitas, Pinot Noir (2006)
This comes from a family-owned estate in Marlborough. The winery has been around since the early 1990s, and they've learned a few things about making fantastic Pinot Noir. This is a complicated mélange of spices, raspberries, cherries, and even a bit of game. It spends 10 months in oak and already carries itself with considerable elegance.
#19818
Original Price: $27.99
Sale Price: $23.79

9. Sileni Sauvignon Blanc (2007)
This New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc offers everything we look for in a fresh white from this neck of the woods. It is lightly herbaceous, with upfront gooseberry fruit, juicy acidity, and perfect ripeness. While this is a dry wine, it is absolutely packed to the brim with fruit.
#12522
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

8. Huia Sauvignon Blanc (2007)
Gooseberry, tropical fruit, and lime notes abound in this textbook New Zealand Sauvignon Blanc. A classic wine calls for a classic pairing, so try it with shellfish for a phenomenal experience.
#12240
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

7. Craggy Range "Sophia" Gimblett Gravels (2004)
Very Bordelais in style, with an additional dose of dark berried fruits on the palate. Elegant yet full-bodied and certainly a wine you can cellar for years.
#19810
Original Price: $55.99
Sale Price: $47.59

6. Felton Road, Chardonnay (2006)
New Zealand's Felton Road winery is located in Bannockburn, Central Otago, the most southerly wine growing region in the world. Their Chardonnay is toasty and dry; on the palate it's full of apples, citrus, and hazelnut fruits.
#20542
Original Price: $36.99
Sale Price: $31.44

5. Kim Crawford Sauvignon Blanc (2007)
This white shows lush tropical fruit aromas with key lime and a touch of green bell pepper. The round fruit is evenly balanced with good acidity on the palate to keep everything fresh.
#10715
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

4. Seresin Chardonnay (2005)
These Chardonnay grapes are hand-harvested and vinified using natural yeasts. The winery uses 20% new oak and ages for thirteen months in barrel. The resulting wine is both smoky and citrus-laden, with lots of ripe fruit on the nose and palate. It's the kind of wine to have with hearty chicken and pork dishes.
#20589
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

3. Cloudy Bay Pinot Noir (2005)
With plums, dark fruits, exotic spice, and toasty oak, this sophisticated wine is highly sought after by those who have discovered the wonders of New Zealand Pinot Noir.
#10952
Original Price: $41.99
Sale Price: $35.69

2. Wellies Sauvignon Blanc, Marlborough (2007)
The Wairau Valley in Marlborough is New Zealand's original and most famous area for Sauvignon Blanc. The Wellies bottling is incredibly approachable, and this new vintage has just arrived on our shelves here at Astor. The nose is full of gooseberries, fresh herbs and melon. An incredibly fresh, dry white wine to suit any number of occasions.
#20366
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $9.99

1. Ata Rangi Pinot Noir, Martinborough (2006)
This delicious red wine comes from the region of Martinborough, at the southern tip of the North Island of New Zealand. The producers of this Pinot Noir were highly concerned with preserving varietal typicity, and the wine exhibits beautiful redcurrant and red cherry fruit flavors.
#13130
Original Price: $41.99
Sale Price: $35.69
