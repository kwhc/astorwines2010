Our Top 11
South African Wines
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Excelsior Shiraz - 2005 (750 mL)
A juicy, tasty red from Robertson, South Africa. It shows loads of dark fruit on the palate with cassis and a touch of smoke on the finish. The tannins are eager to please - in fact, this wine just won't let you down in general. Friendly and approachable.
#14507
Original Price: $7.99
Sale Price: $6.79

10. Thelema Merlot Stellenbosch - 2005 (750 mL)
Thelema has become one of the stars of South African wine. Their Merlot is richly perfumed with coffee and ripe plum flavors, and remarkably friendly tannins. This bottle is highly sought after; taste why!
#70154
Original Price: $27.99
Sale Price: $23.79

9. Solms Wijn de Caab "Lekkerwijn" Rosé - 2006 (750 mL)
The Solms winery is located in the Franschhoek Valley, an area with a Mediterranean climate where Rhône grape varieties thrive. Barrel-aged and made from Grenache, Mourvèdre, and Viognier, this wine belongs in the "serious rosé" category: it delivers nuanced complexity that rewards attentive sipping.
#19830
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

8. De Wetshof Estate "Lesca" Chardonnay - 2007 (750 mL)
A delicious white wine that is lightly oaked for 3 months; the wood shows up on the palate as a fresh undertone of vanilla bean. With its abundant floral apple fruit, you'd never mistake this for anything but a great Chardonnay. It's drinking well today but could certainly hold for a few years to come.
#16617
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

7. The William, Graham Beck - 2003 (750 mL)
A delicious and complex blend of Pinotage and Cabernet Sauvignon. This dark red shows lots of spicy oak and cassis on the nose and palate.
#15193
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

6. Cape Indaba Chenin Blanc - 2007 (750 mL)
One of Astor's best white wine values, this Chenin Blanc has tantalizing aromas of ripe pears and flowers, with exuberant tropical fruit on the palate. This is great with cheeses, fish, and light pasta dishes.
#70010
Original Price: $5.99
Sale Price: $5.09

5. Hamilton Russell Pinot Noir - 2007 (750 mL)
Hamilton Russell Vineyards concentrates on only two grape varieties - Chardonnay and Pinot Noir - because they believe these are best suited for the terroir of Walker Bay, South Africa. It's ruby in color, with aromas of cherries and warm “winter” spices like vanilla and cinnamon.
#11043
Original Price: $36.99
Sale Price: $31.44

4. Sauvignon Blanc, Buitenverwachting - 2007 (750 mL)
We won't even attempt to sound the name out phonetically, but we will tell you its translation: "beyond expectation." We found that very apt when we tried this wine. Made partly from vine plantings that are over 80 years old, with some younger ones mixed in as well, it has notes of crisp fresh flowers, peppers and minerals. Still fresh and young, this will evolve wonderfully with a bit of bottle age. From Constantia, a cooler-climate ward of the coastal region of South Africa.
#14547
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

3. Brampton Shiraz - 2006 (750 mL)
A solid Shiraz from South Africa. The Brampton line of wines from the Stellenbosch region are made in a style that boasts upfront fruit on the palate. This is unoaked and shows all the red, peppery fruit you've come to love in this versatile grape.
#14014
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

2. Pinotage, Graham Beck - 2008 (750 mL)
Here's a wonderful introduction to the world of Pinotage - or a great way to continue your exploration if you're already familiar with the variety. Pinotage is a unique South African hybrid of Cinsault and Pinot Noir that is medium-bodied and easy-drinking.
#20885
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

1. Mulderbosch Sauvignon Blanc, Stellenbosch - 2007 (750 mL)
An absolutely beautiful, world-class Sauvignon Blanc. It's very crisp with layers of delicious ripe melon and mineral flavors. Serve with a simple fish dish and let this wine shine.
#77184
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99
