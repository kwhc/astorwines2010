Our Top 11
Piedmont Wines
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Gavi, Cà Adua - 2007
There are some insipid bottles of Gavi out there, but this isn't one of them: the Cà Adua is the type of white wine we get excited about. The nose offers pure and precise aromas of yellow fruit, oregano, fresh herbs, and wet stones. On the palate the fruit is rich, satisfying, and intense. An absolute treat that we were delighted to find.
#21091
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

10. Brachetto d'Acqui "Rebecca", Nuova Abbazia di Vallechiara - 2007
A dessert wine that's just slightly sweet on the palate. The color is a show-stopping, luminescent cherry red. The fruit is all raspberries and mixed wild berries, and it finishes with a mineral edge. A bit of light carbonation only adds to this wine's appeal.
#19627
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

9. Pelaverga, Vigna di Terre Rosse - 2007
This is a beautiful, bright crimson-colored wine made from the rare Pelaverga grape. It is shy upon opening, but within a couple of hours begins to show fresh cranberry fruit with vivid aromas of flowers, spice, clay, and powder. Delicately textured with a subtle iron minerality on the finish. Just another humble yet amazing Piedmontese wine, well worthy of a patient taster's attention. This comes from the Saluzzo area (southwest of Turin), which is known as the original home of Pelaverga.
#13001
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

8. Barbera d'Alba "Sori del Drago", Brovia - 2006
Medium-bodied with voluptuous plum fruit. As is so typical of Barbera, this is dangerously easy-drinking. A bottle for those who crave a red with substance without a highly tannic backbone.
#46999
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

7. Barolo "Villero", Brovia - 2000
The Villero is a heavier, denser Barolo from Brovia that is nicely nuanced. It has a fine minerality, along with delicate aromas of roses, red berries, and hazelnuts. All in all a very impressive Nebbiolo.
#10953
Original Price: $64.99
Sale Price: $55.24

6. Dogliani "Papa Celso", Abbona - 2006
This is the Abbona family's "tre bicchieri"-winning Dolcetto, produced from older vines. It is the pride of their estate, showing lovely, bright red fruit, elegance, and the classic soft femininity of Dolcetto.
#10381
Original Price: $26.99
Sale Price: $22.94

5. Erbaluce "Cariola," Ferrando - 2007
The "Cariola" is a medium-bodied, fragrant, still white wine from Caluso in northern Piedmont. Made from Erbaluce, an indigenous white grape variety, this has ripe, white floral aromas that lead to a very lovely mineral finish.
#10417
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

4. Grignolino, Cascina Tavijn - 2006
Grignolino is a rare red Italian grape, and it's simply a fun wine to drink with food. This bottle has loads of red cherry fruit to it. It's dry, pale red in color, and works wonders with antipasti.
#12401
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. Timorasso "Sassobraglia", Fontanassa, Val Borbera - 2007
The Timorasso grape is a specialty of southern Piedmont. This one comes from the Valley of Borbera, which separates Piedmont from Liguria. The wine has lovely, bright acidity and delightful pear skin notes on the nose.
#21092
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

2. Monferrato Dolcetto "Nibio", Cascina degli Ulivi - 2005
"Nibio" is an ancient name for Dolcetto and hearkens back to a time when the grape was widely planted around Gavi (those vineyards are now dedicated to the Cortese variety). This dark, earthy, and mineral-laden rendition of Dolcetto offers a fresh look at a grape known for its ebullient fruitiness. This 16-hectare estate is not only Biodynamic but also self-sustaining, in the sense that they raise everything from chickens to the yeasts they use in fermentation. Their wines are weirdly beautiful, compelling, and entirely worth experimenting with. The Dolcetto comes from the so-called "Terre Rosse": red, iron-rich soils that impart a distinctive mineral character to this wine.
#21389
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

1. "Montemarino" VdT, Cascina degli Ulivi - 2007
Made from 120-year-old Cortese vines, this is arguably the best Gavi-style wine you'll ever have. The nose gives up dense white fruit and floral aromas, while the palate is rich and packed with ripe, succulent flavors. A bottle not to be missed.
#21350
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54
