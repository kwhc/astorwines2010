Astor's Top Tuscan Wines
(our lists go up to 11)


11. Rosso di Montalcino, La Palazzetta - 2007
La Palazzetta consistently produces extraordinary Tuscan red wines. Made from organically farmed grapes and aged in large Slavonian oak casks, this bottle shows all of Sangiovese's pure, tangy berry character.
#10090
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

10. Antinori Villa Red IGT - 2004
Red berries and light oaky aromas on this red. Unobtrusive tannins make it go well with a huge range of meat dishes.
#47174
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

9. Chianti Classico Riserva, Val delle Corti - 2004
The aromas on this Sangiovese are full of dark fruits, licorice and mixed berries with a palate of elegance and balance. This was aged in bottle for six months before release. While young and accessible, it certainly can be held for years to come.
#18325
Original Price: $30.99
Sale Price: $26.34

8. Vernaccia, Montenidoli Fiore - 2006
This Vernaccia from the hilltop town of San Gimignano has notes of ripe lemons and melon, while remaining dry on the finish. This wine is very fresh and is great as an apéritif or with lighter fare.
#48454
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

7. Monte Antico Rosso - 2005
From the hills of southern Tuscany comes this Sangiovese-based red. Easy drinking for everyday meals.
#45484
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

6. "Casamatta", Bibi Graetz - 2007
When we first tasted this delightful, unoaked Sangiovese, we found it to be ripe, earthy, and fruity in all the right places. Bibi Graetz has garnered much international acclaim and we're beginning to understand why.
#21549
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

5. Rosso di Montalcino, Uccelliera - 2006
This estate is located in the southern part of the Montalcino DOCG and was formerly part of the Ciacci Piccolomini's holdings. The wines are big and bold, though far from opaque, and filled with dark cherry fruit.
#17975
Original Price: $25.99
Sale Price: $22.09

4. Brunello di Montalcino, Talenti - 2003
This estate was founded in the 1980s by Pierluigi Talenti from Emilia Romagna, who had long been in love with Tuscany. Today these 40 hectares produce some of the most traditional and profound wines of the region.
#20339
Original Price: $59.99
Sale Price: $50.99

3. Il Sodaccio, Montevertine - 1995
When you're looking to go back to basics in Tuscany, stick with classic estates like this one. This bottle is just coming around but could truly be held for well over a decade longer. Full of aromas of black cherries, blackberries, violets, clay, and thyme. Made from old Sangiovese vines that have since been replanted. Only time will tell if Il Sodaccio will ever be made again.
#16334
Original Price: $59.99
Sale Price: $50.99

2. Vino Nobile di Montelpulciano, Bindella - 2005
CO. A lovely combination of plum aromas, leather, tobacco and a bit of earth. This red spends just a bit of time in oak adding a modern touch yet preserving the aromas of the grapes we cherish. Enjoy with rich fare. Will benefit from air.
#11102
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

1. Etrusco - Etrurian, Cennatoio - 2001
Concentrated, elegant, and rich, this 100% Sangiovese Grosso has exquisite blackberry aromas, with notes of smoke, leather and anise. It drinks well today with decanting, but will certainly evolve and improve with age. This is aged in 50% new barrique, but the oak is unobtrusive on the palate.
#46006
Original Price: $39.99
Sale Price: $33.99
