Deep, golden tropical fruit is the theme here. Full-bodied and sublimely flavorful, this wine is incredibly rich yet fully dry, showing great balancing acidity.

10. Pinot Blanc, Mittnacht - 2007
Pale peach and pear flavors play against a hint of minerality on this white wine from Alsace. It finishes long, with a gentle kiss of sweetness.

9. Riesling "Clos de la Trielle" Baur - 2005
From Baur's granite-rich monopole comes this delightfully complex bottle. Stone fruits so ripe they give the illusion of sweetness dance on the nose and palate, giving way to a fully dry wine with bright acidity and a tightly wound mineral finish.

8. Gentil, Hugel - 2007
This dry wine is made from a blend of white grapes, and has a pleasing mix of spicy and floral characteristics. It is a great match for pâté or trout.
#21784
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $10.99

7. Riesling, Mittnacht - 2007
This delightfully rich and expressive Riesling displays all the body and minerality that give Alsatian wines their charm. Made on a 20-hectare estate run by two conscientious brothers, this dense, balanced, and lively wine is full of petrol and ripe orchard fruits on the nose and palate.
#21595

Sale Price: $17.84

6. Riesling "Katzenthal," Binner - 2004
This unfiltered Riesling is unexpectedly full in body. It shows the classic peach-and-apricot fruit profile of the best examples of this varietal, but there's a good dose of citrus in there as well. Organic.

Sale Price: $18.69

5. Pinot Blanc "Herrenweg", Baur - 2006
Aromas of white peach and pear greet the nose; this is a soft, easy-drinking white wine with a full, aromatic bouquet.

4. Pinot Noir "Cuvée M", Laurent Barth - 2006
In the right hands, the red Pinot Noir grape can even thrive in Alsace. Only a minute amount of "Cuvée M" is produced, but we managed to get our hands on some. Full of currants, lithe tannins, and all-around approachable fruit character.
#17338
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

3. Gewurztraminer, Binner - 2005
The aromatics here are a bit reserved, making this a great starter Gewürztraminer. This has honeydew and floral undertones on the nose; the palate is dry, with the characteristic viscosity of Alsatian white wines.
#21300

Sale Price: $23.79

2. Riesling, W. Gisselbrecht - 2007
Here we have an off-dry, fruit-driven white wine that is extremely easy to appreciate. This is great before dinner as an apéritif, and also works well with spicy meals.
#18939
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

1. Pinot, "Cépage Pinot Noir" Blanc, Jean Pierre Dirler Richard Hamilton Viognier - 2006
This is an extremely rare find: a white wine made from Pinot Noir grapes. By immediately separating the grape skins from the juice after crushing, Dirler prevents the finished wine from being imbued with the full red color of the skins. The result is this very dry, faintly copper-tinged wine, which has an earthy nose and a near-saline minerality that emerges with a bit of exposure to air. This bottle is an uncommon treat that is well worth seeking out.
#20080
Original Price: $32.99
Sale Price: $28.04
