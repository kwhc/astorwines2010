Our Top 11
Wines of Provence and Languedoc-Roussillon
(... our lists go up to 11)


11. Minervois, Ch. d'Oupia - 2006 (750 mL)
An aromatic and densely colored red wine, full-bodied with generous blackberry and raspberry flavors. Fine tannins, along with accents of pepper and spice, make this an elegant and well-balanced wine.
#26394
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

10. Bandol Rouge, Domaine Tempier - 2006 (750 mL)
This beautiful Provençal offering is reminiscent of sun-ripened dark fruit and the spice of garrigue. The wine is 75% Mourvèdre, blended with Grenache, Cinsault, and Carignan. Though a touch austere in its youth, this will flesh out beautifully over the next couple years. Drink it with wild boar.
#11902
Original Price: $39.99
Sale Price: $33.99

9. Côtes de Provence "Escapade", Comptoirs de Magdala - 2006 (750 mL)
Antoine Pouponneau, winemaker of Tour du Bon Bandol, has created this brilliant Côtes de Provence. It's full of bright red fruit and balanced acidity.
#16570
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

8. Côte de Provence "Cuvée Carolle" Dom de la Sauveuse - 2006 (750 mL)
A hearty red blend - Cabernet, Grenache, Syrah, and Mourvèdre - from the south of France. This is full of earthy dark berries and a touch of peppermint from oak aging. Excellent with hard cheeses and cured meats.
#20359
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

7. Muscat Sec, Mas las Cabes, Dom. Gardies - 2007 (750 mL)
While the Muscat grape is often vinified in a sweet style, Languedoc winemakers excel at making dry versions like this one. Put just a light chill on this bottle and appreciate all it has to offer: we especially liked its delicate bouquet of white flowers, fresh limes, and white pepper.
#12663
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

6. Picpoul de Pinet "Gaujal de St. Bon," Gaujal - 2007 (750 mL)
An exciting and unusual white wine made in southwest France from the Picpoul Blanc grape. Light and dry, this has lemony aromas, raw honey flavors on the palate, and even a nice little kick of mineral on the finish. The Mediterranean climate of this appellation gives its wines plenty of acidity, and this one goes well with a huge range of foods. It's tasty on its own, too.
#12378
Original Price: $10.99
Sale Price: $9.34

5. Pichon Viognier - 2007 (750 mL)
This red wine is made from the Gamay grape variety. On the nose you'll find hints of black pepper, followed by ample fresh cherry fruit on the palate.
#13806
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

4. Minervois "La Mère Grand", Le Loup Blanc - 2004 (750 mL)
If you've ever been curious about what Viognier can be, try this rich, round white wine. Thrilling aromas of peaches and pears, along with a long, smooth finish, make this a revelation - especially if you are unacquainted with this grape. Perfect with poultry.
#18122
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

3. Pic Saint-Loup "Les Tonillières," Mas Foulaquier - 2005 (750 mL)
This Pic Saint-Loup comes from southern France's Coteaux du Languedoc region. It’s a full-bodied, unoaked, dry red blend that bursts with mixed dark fruit aromas.
#18279
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

2. VdP de Vaucluse, Dom. de Fenouillet - 2007 (750 mL)
This tasty red wine is from the southern borders of the Rhône Valley, more specifically known as the Vaucluse. This based on the Marcelan grape (a cross between Grenache and Cabernet Sauvignon), with Merlot and Alicante Bouschet. Naturally, there's a bit of spice on the palate and lots of mixed dark fruit.
#15777
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

1. Costières de Nîmes "Marginal", Terre des Chardons - 2006 (750 mL)
A delicious, concentrated red wine that's left unoaked in order to emphasize the character of the grapes. This is a new arrival at Astor, and we couldn't be more pleased with the quality of this voluptuous wine. It's very pure, with lots of dark fruits (currants, black cherries, blackberries) and floral spices on the finish. Feel free to decant this for half an hour before drinking.
#20227
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54
