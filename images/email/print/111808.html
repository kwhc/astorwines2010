our top 11:
Alsatian Wines
(... our lists go up to 11)


11. Gewürz "Wintzenheim" Zind-Humbrecht - 2006 (375 mL)
Old vines increase the concentration of this wine, while young vines add to its balance. The result is brimful of stunning floral and lychee aromas, with a touch of residual sugar. Botrytis flavors and aromas are definitely present here, too.
#20513
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

10. Riesling, W. Gisselbrecht - 2006 (750 mL)
Here we have an off-dry, fruit-driven white wine that is extremely easy to appreciate. This is great before dinner as an apéritif, and also works well with spicy meals.
#18939
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

9. Gentil, Hugel - 2006 (750 mL)
This dry white wine is made from a blend of grapes, and has a pleasing mix of spicy and floral characteristics. It is a great match for pâté or trout.
#21784
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

8. Riesling Grand Cru "Altenberg de Bergbieten", Frédéric Mochel - 2005 (750 mL)
Abundant apple flavor and juicy peach scents characterize this Riesling. It's fun to drink, but at the same time shows plenty of seriousness; it richly deserves its Grand Cru designation.
#19010
Original Price: $36.99
Sale Price: $31.44

7. Gewürztraminer, Trimbach - 2005 (750 mL)
Classic Alsatian Gewürztraminer: dry, elegant, and mineral, with fine floral aromas. A very nice bottle, and an excellent introduction to one of the most delicious and distinctive grape varieties there is.
#28594
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

6. Pinot Noir "Cuveé Ludivine", Jean Pierre Dirler - 2005 (750 mL)
Dirler is an expert when it comes to handling Pinot Noir from Alsace. This is his "best in show" - and you'll taste why the minute you open the bottle.
#20081
Original Price: $34.99
Sale Price: $29.74

5. Riesling "Cuvée Albert", Albert Mann - 2007 (750 mL)
Albert Mann Domaine is a joint venture between two families in Alsace: their goal is to create elegant, terroir-driven wines. This Riesling is well balanced, with ripe fruit and bold minerality.
#17470
Original Price: $25.99
Sale Price: $22.09

4. Tokay Pinot Gris "Brand", Boxler - 2005 (750 mL)
For some odd reason, Pinot Gris is referred to as "Tokay Pinot Gris" in Alsace - much to the distress of Hungarians, who cherish an entirely different type of "Tokaji" wine. This dry white comes from the Brand vineyard site, a Grand Cru. It's quite rich, with aromas of red currants, spice, and peaches.
#19443
Original Price: $59.99
Sale Price: $50.99

3. Pinot Gris "Lerchenberg", Kreydenweiss - 2006 (750 mL)
Pinot Gris is a mutation of Pinot Noir that produces greyish-brown berries and yields round, full-bodied white wines. Try this one the next time you're looking for an alternative to rich, unoaked Chardonnay. Pinot Gris thrives in Alsace, especially in the hands of genius Biodynamic winemaker Marc Kreydenweiss.
#20662
Original Price: $35.99
Sale Price: $30.59

2. Sylvaner "Sono Contento", A. Seltz - 1996 (750 mL; you'll find this item in our Cool Room)
The Sylvaner grape renders white wines with an earthy, floral appeal that we've appreciated for years. Add to that the remarkable weather in this vintage and you'll be astounded at how this relatively unknown white grape can perform with age.
#19947
Original Price: $47.99
Sale Price: $40.79

1. Pinot Blanc "Cuvée George", Ginglinger - 2006 (750 mL)
A lush, golden Pinot blanc that is full of white flower aromas and peach flavors. This complex and fruit-driven white wine is great with shellfish or white meats - try it with roast turkey for a seamless pairing.
#17745
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74
