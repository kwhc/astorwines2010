Astor's Top Beaujolais Wines
Our lists go to 11.


11. Beaujolais Blanc, Ch. du Chatelard - 2007
No, your eyes aren't deceiving you: this is indeed a white Beaujolais. Chardonnay is by far in the minority when it comes to wine production in this region. This one is unoaked, ripe, and pleasantly round in texture. It has also gained incredible concentration from the impressive age of the vines. Give it a try tonight!
#19462
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

10. Brouilly Vieilles Vignes, Lapalu - 2007
On the palate this Gamay is showing the finesse and ease that the best Brouillys offer. With dark cherries, violets, and a wet, stony finish, this is just brilliant alongside sausages, ham, and country pâtés.
#13503
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

9. Beaujolais-Villages "La Lutine", Dom. des Terres Vivantes - 2007
This wine is an irresistibly lively pink-hued ruby red in the glass, a color that captures the spirit of the wine. On the nose and palate, the wine gives plenty of juicy Gamay fruit along with a healthy dose of granite mineral on the finish. The name "Lutine" means "little elfin girl" - which seems an apt image to associate with this light, joyous wine. The estate farms sustainably with no chemical pesticides, and uses only natural yeasts.
#20993
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

8. Chiroubles, C. Pacalet - 2006
A wine of character with distinct terroir. It has an earthy edge, but it’s the fruit that shines here: ripe raspberries, orange rind, cherry, and a touch of musk. Very fresh and very pretty, yet maintaining a mien of rusticity, the wine is produced organically, with no sulphur added.
#11953
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

7. Morgon VV, Daniel Bouland - 2007
One of the 10 Crus of the Beaujolais region, Morgon is noted for producing wines with darker, more powerful fruit profiles than are typical of the Gamay grape. This one shows excellent character with a rustic, earthy edge that pairs well with the charcuterie (sausages, hams, country pâtés) so widely enjoyed in the wine's region of origin – not to mention here in NYC.
#14222
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

6. Saint-Amour, Dom. des Billards - 2007
A light and easy-drinking Saint-Amour that is full of red fruit and savory, gamey notes. If you've never tried a wine from this northernmost Beaujolais Cru, now is the time.
#21749
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

5. Chénas, C. Pacalet - 2006
The northern part of Beaujolais is divided into ten "Crus," the smallest of which is known as Chénas. Christophe Pacalet's Chénas bottling, made from Gamay, is a touch gamey, with pure aromas of freshly crushed mixed berries. This is a wine that cries out for food.
#17123
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $15.99

4. Régnié, Georges Descombes - 2008
This wine is made by the 5th member of the legendary Morgon Gang of Four... it's his Régnié, which goes through a shorter raising process, making for a fruitier, young-drinking wine. Elegant, floral, and full of exuberant Gamay fruit, this is a bottle to chill down and drink on a warm summer evening.
#21707
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

3. Morgon "Côte du Py", Foillard - 2007
Coming from Morgon's famous hillside vineyard of the Côte du Py, widely considered to produce superior wines, this Morgon possesses sweet black fruit notes while an ethereal body keeps it light and silky on the palate. Jean Foillard produces beautiful, delicate wine year after year.
#11864
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

2. Fleurie, Dom. des Grands Fers - 2007
A fantastic Cru Beaujolais from a phenomenal vintage, with lots of black fruit aromas and violets. It's just slightly tannic, and while this is a red wine to drink today, it certainly has enough structure to hold up to rich meat dishes.
#13452
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.99

1. Brouilly, Ch. des Tours - 2007
Chateau des Tours has been producing particularly fine wines of late. This vintage takes full advantage of their 40-year-old vines. Direct and full on the nose with classic notes of violets, black cherry, and moist earth. The slightly solid structure of the vintage supports crisp red berry flavors in the mouth, with a touch of fresh herbs. Lovely now but will improve with short-term aging.
#20644
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44
