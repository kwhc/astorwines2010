our top 11:
Wines of Chile
(... we go to eleven)

11. Manta Sauvignon Blanc (2007)
A light, easy-drinking wine made even brighter by fresh melon and lemon zest flavors. This is a purely refreshing white wine that is a hit at parties, so consider picking up a case.
#12655
Original Price: $6.99
Sale Price: $5.94

10. Concha y Toro, Casillero del Diablo, Cab. Sauvignon (2007)
This wine starts off with a good amount of wood, but then rounds out into a nicely ripe, well-balanced, fruity finish that is uncommon in any Cabernet Sauvignon at this price.
#54204
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

9. Cousiño-Macul Cabernet Sauvignon (2006)
Excellent quality from one of the most consistent wineries in Chile. Full-bodied, dark-fruited, and earthy with ripe tannins.
#54744
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

8. Los Vascos Cabernet Sauvignon (2005)
A straightforward Cabernet Sauvignon from the Chilean property of Domaine Lafite Rothschild. It's extremely approachable, with ripe cassis fruit and soft tannins to please anyone's palate..
#54434
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

7. Viña Chocolan, Carmenère (2007)
Carmenère is a grape that originated in Bordeaux but is rarely planted today - except in Chile, where it seems to have found its niche. The resulting wines are fabulous: for proof, try this dark, full-bodied, and delicious everyday red.
#19317
Original Price: $10.99
Sale Price: $9.34

6. Santa Rita Cabernet Sauvignon Medalla Réal (2006)
A sophisticated, brooding Cabernet from Chile. It shows plenty of dark fruit, with toasty notes and subtle crushed mint. It's incredible with beef braises.
#54844
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

5. Chono Carménère (2006)
This is a hearty, dark red wine made from the Carmenère grape variety. It's aged in oak for a short period of time to give it just a little spice on the palate. An excellent choice for anything barbecued or meat-based.
#17923
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

4. Cono Sur Pinot Noir, Vision (2007)
We're always pleased to taste a Pinot Noir like this one from Cono: bright, fruity, and varietally correct in so many ways. Aromas of raspberries and cherries dominate the palate. The abundant fruit makes this as clear an expression of New World Pinot Noir as anything on our shelves.
#19811
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

3. Terra Noble Gran Reserva Carmenère (2007)
The Carmenère grape came to Chile via Bordeaux in the late nineteenth century, and lately it's been more at home in South America than France. The red wines made from this grape can range from refreshing and easy-drinking to full-bodied and dense. This is one of the brawnier ones, with rich, dark fruit and a touch of spiciness from oak aging.
#17595
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

2. Amayna, Sauvignon Blanc, Leyda Valley (2006)
Leyda, a sub-region of San Antonio, is located in a valley between the Coastal Mountain Range and the Pacific Ocean, running parallel to Chile's Central Valley. This wine has lots of tropical fruit, dominated by white pineapple and white flowers. A dry, distinctively New World Sauvignon Blanc.
#19470
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

1. Casa Lapostolle Merlot "Cuvée Alexandre" (2005)
Rich and lovely, this beautiful expression of Merlot is produced in Chile. Its plummy fruit is wrapped in a velvety-smooth texture that only the best Merlots exhibit. Enjoy this with beef stews and braises.
#58154
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69
