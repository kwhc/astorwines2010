Our Top 11
Wines of Portugal
(... our lists go up to 11)


11. Tinto da Anfora, Herdade das Anforas - 2006 (750 mL)
Made from a blend of red grapes in southern Portugal's Alentejo region, this is aged in oak for 12 months. Loaded with dark berry fruit, it is a purely tasty, unfussy wine that is easy to drink and easy to love.
#18153
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

10. Monte das Ânforas, Herdade das Ânforas - 2007 (750 mL)
This hearty red wine comes from southern Portugal's Alentejo region. Based on the Aragónez grape (a.k.a. Tempranillo), with some Trincadeira and Alfrocheiro blended in, it is full of mixed black berry flavors. Unoaked and showing incredible fruit concentration, this is drinking terrifically well today.
#17701
Original Price: $7.99
Sale Price: $6.79

9. Vinho Verde, Provam - 2007 (750 mL)
This tasty white comes from the area of Monção in the Vinho Verde region of northern Portugal. It’s dry on the palate, offering firm minerals and notes of tarragon and green apples. A natural match for anything seafood-based, but also very refreshing all by itself on a warm spring day.
#16348
Original Price: $6.99
Sale Price: $5.94

8. Serras de Azeitão, Vinho Regional Terras do Sado - 2007 (750 mL)
This beautiful dry white wine is driven by floral fruits and spicy pear flavors. Unoaked and lightly mineral-driven on the finish, this is a fine choice if you're having seafood tonight. Made in the Terras do Sado region, just south of Lisbon in southern Portugal, this is a blend of indigenous white grapes and a little bit of Chardonnay.
#19450
Original Price: $6.99
Sale Price: $5.94

7. Santa Fé de Arraiolos, Vinho Regional Alentejano - 2006 (750 mL)
An excellent red if you're looking for something dark, rich, and dripping with black fruit flavors. This is a fresh and lightly tannic wine, and it shines with a huge range of meat-based cuisines.
#19451
Original Price: $7.99
Sale Price: $6.79

6. Meia Pipa, Vinho Regional Terras do Sado - 2006 (750 mL)
A Portuguese treat for lovers of big, plush Napa Valley reds. In this bottle you'll find plenty of voluptuous dark berry flavor and outstanding freshness - a characteristic that too often plays second fiddle to oak in modern wines.
#20555
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

5. Quinta do Crasto, Douro - 2006 (750 mL)
This red wine speaks of Portugal. It's plush with dark fruits, and espresso foam. Very rich and full of lively character on the palate. A great match for meat fare.
#13806
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

4. Quinta do Monte d'Oiro, Viognier "Madrigal" - 2006 (750 mL)
This is a brilliant bottle for the lover of rich, round Viognier from the Rhône. Hints of pear skin mingle with creamy oak nuances on the nose and palate. Despite it's heft, the wine has solid acidity and would pair delightfully with roast chicken.
#20627
Original Price: $46.99
Sale Price: $39.94

3. Cardeal Reserva, Touriga Nacional, Dão - 2002 (750 mL)
Made from Touriga Nacional, this fresh red wine shows firm dark fruit, warm spices, and a gentle touch of fresh tobacco on the finish. It has a great tannic structure that allows it to hold up to big, meaty dishes.
#18279
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

2. Vinha Barrio, Luis Pato - 2001 (750 mL)
An earthy red that's medium-bodied and full of red berried fruit. Made from the Baga indigenous grape variety. Another delcious bottle of wine from the excellent estate of Luis Pato.
#15974
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

1. Auratus, Alvarinho-Trajadura - 2007 (750 mL)
A blend of Alvarinho and Trajadura, Auratus is a Portuguese white that goes beyond your traditional Vinho Verde. Very clean and fresh, this has notes of honey and ripe apples, with vivid acidity and mineral notes.
#12427
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59
