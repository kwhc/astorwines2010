our top 11:
California Red Zinfandel
(... we go to eleven)

11. Sea Star Zinfandel (2004)
We just can’t get enough of this spicy red wine. It is bursting with aromas of candied blackberries and cherries, and its ripe, luscious style makes it a perfect match for anything from burgers to pizza.
#16017
Sale Price: $5.94

10. Benson Ferry Old Vine Zinfandel (2006)
What does "Old Vine" mean? In this case, we’re talking 50-year-old Zinfandel vines. This bottling from Benson Ferry is the product of three generations of winemaking in the Stokes Family. This juicy red wine is all about blackberries and blueberries, with a subtle nuance of cedar and mint from a moderate six months of oak aging.
#12243
Sale Price: $11.04

9. Wild Oaks Old Vine Zinfandel, St. Francis (2004)
Most of the grapes for this Zinfandel come from a volcanic ridge high about Sonoma Valley. The wine shows intense, juicy berry flavors and a balanced acidity. Underlying notes of baking spices and a toasted vanilla round out the palate.
#18646
Sale Price: $25.49

8. Mauritson Zinfandel, Dry Creek (2006)
The Mauritsons have been growing grapes in Sonoma since the 1870s. Beginning with the 1998 vintage, the family (led by winemaker Clay Mauritson) has been making its own wine. The fruit for this Dry Creek Zinfandel comes from four different vineyard sites, creating a wine that is complex and well balanced, with notes of dark chocolate and black plum.
#14054
Sale Price: $21.24

7. Zinfandel, Seghesio (2006)
In 1895, the Seghesio family came to Sonoma and planted their first Zinfandel vines. This Sonoma Zinfandel is a collage of fruit from four estate-owned vineyards and exhibits briar, spice, and rich berry character.
#13547
Sale Price: $16.99

6. Cline Zinfandel Ancient Vines (2006)
This mouth-coating Zinfandel offers flavors of toasted caramel and dark berries. With soft tannins and a lasting vanilla finish, the Ancient Vine Zin is dangerously easy drinking.
#65011
Sale Price: $15.99

5. Klinker Brick Old Vines Zinfandel (2005)
This hearty, full-bodied red comes from Zinfandel vines that are well over 50 years old. The wine shows intense concentration, packed with berry flavors set off by spicy oak, and aromas of dark fruit contrasting with fresh herbal notes.
#18161
Sale Price: $14.44

4.Quivira Zinfandel, Dry Creek (2005)
Quivira is a small, organic, family-run estate in the heart of Sonoma. This Dry Creek Zinfandel is produced with fruit coming from a few different vineyards throughout the Dry Creek Valley. It shows bright red raspberry fruit, with underlying layers of tobacco, cedar, and mocha.
#15225
Sale Price: $16.99

3. Ridge Zinfandel Lytton Springs (2005)
From winemaker Paul Draper comes a truly iconic Zinfandel. For several decades, the Lytton Springs vineyard in Sonoma County has been the source for these well-balanced, pure, and age-worthy bottlings. While rambunctious in their youth, a few years of cellaring brings out layers of flavors and an elegant quality very rarely found in California Zinfandel.
#63264
Sale Price: $28.89

2. Nalle Zinfandel, Dry Creek Valley (2005)
Lovely raspberry aromas and dry, earthy aromatics greet the nose, along with a moderate dose of oak. This medium-bodied Zin shows good acidity and tannins that are supportive yet unobtrusive. It has succulent, intense strawberry fruit on the palate, and a finish that is a touch compressed at the moment. Lingering earth and light toast notes frame the brambly fruit. This is an exceptionally civilized California Zinfandel.
#61284
Sale Price: $24.64

1. Saxon Brown Zinfandel, Casa Santinamaria (2003)
This offers up an incredibly expressive nose, with loads of cedary spice, grape leaf, and tobacco, leading to jammy fruit and a hint of milk chocolate with an earthy underlay. The wine shows an elegantly restrained palate impression for such a warm vintage.
#17218
Sale Price: $29.74
