our top 11:
Sakés
(... we go to eleven)


11. Hanahato Kijoshu Saké, Junmai Koshu
Koshu saké is aged saké. This one has been aged for 8 years before releasing for sale. This aging has a remarkable effect on the aromas. Full of mixed, toasted nuts with a touch of dried fruit and even a little soy.
#72012
Original Price: $44.99
Sale Price: $38.24

10. Hitori Musume Nigori Saké, Junmai Nigori
This nigori (unfiltered) saké has aromas of fresh chestnuts and almonds. Soft and smooth, it boasts lovely hints of yellow apples and freshly baked bread. This is an excellent choice with crab dishes.
#50266
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

9. Tsukinokatsura "Hiyaoroshi" Saké, Junmai Nama
The Tsukinokatsura brewery was the first to brew a nigori saké, and the first to age in ceramic casks. Now they're offering this fall-release saké, and although it isn't their first, it's probably your first experience with a rich, seasonal brew like this one.
#18904
Original Price: $33.99
Sale Price: $28.89

8. Wakatake Onigoroshi "Akino Ki-Ippon" Hiyaoroshi Saké, Junmai
Released once a year in the fall (that's what "Hiyaoroshi" refers to), this is made for immediate consumption. Dry, smooth, and as fresh as well water, with a clean, dry finish. From Shizuoka in Japan and from one of the finest saké producers around.
#11556
Original Price: $33.99
Sale Price: $28.89

7. Dewazakura Saké, Daiginjo
Rich and full of pineapple aromas, this drinks well as an apéritif or alongside rich, meaty fish dishes like halibut. The bottle sports a detail that is quite rare for a saké: the brewer has chosen to list its vintage, indicating when the rice was harvested.
#14114
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

6. Umenishiki Saké, Junmai Daiginjo
A premium saké with exquisitely subtle aromas of fresh white flowers. On the palate, vibrant notes of guava and pineapple. This is dry yet smooth; a perfumed and elegant brew.
#72010
Original Price: $89.99
Sale Price: $76.49

5. Narutotai Genshu, Junmai Ginjo Saké
Refined, unadulterated aromas of ripe green apple and melon. Enjoy this with rich seafood fare.
#56111
Original Price: $46.99
Sale Price: $39.94

4. Kokuryu "500 Mangoku" Saké, Junmai Ginjo
This is quite a dry saké, with a very earth-driven profile that you'll enjoy if you're looking for complexity rather than simple, clean fruit flavors. The "500 Mangoku" refers to the particular rice strain used to brew this saké. This comes from the north of Japan, from a brewery called Kokuryu.
#17983
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $27.19

3. Tsukasabotan Saké, Junmai
From Kochi prefecture on Shikoku Island, Tsukasabotan produces rich saké showing sharp flavors of tropical fruits, Japanese pear, lemons, limes, and green apples on the nose and palate. This pleasantly dry saké can be served chilled or warm, and pairs well a with wide range of meat dishes.
#14540
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

2. Kira Honjozo Saké
Nutty and rich on the palate, this is a saké that tastes great when it's heated up.
#40604
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

1. Nishinoseki Tezukuri Saké, Junmai
From Oita Prefecture. A rich, full-bodied, and off-dry saké (SMV rating of -3) with a smooth, mellow taste and full flavor of rice, nuts, and cocoa. Best when served warm for added complexity and structure, but can also be chilled.
#59013
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49
