Our Top 11
California Syrahs & Zinfandels
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Ravenswood Zinfandel, Vintners Blend - 2006 (750 mL)
This is Ravenswood's introductory Zinfandel. A full-bodied red with black berry fruit, cherries, and pepper aromas. If you like this Zin, try the rest of their wines: you'll immediately see the family resemblance.
#64744
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

10. Klinker Brick, Old Vines Zinfandel - 2006 (750 mL)
This hearty, full-bodied red comes from Zinfandel vines that are well over 50 years old. Aromas of dark fruit contrast with fresh herbal notes; this also shows intense concentration, packed with berry flavors set off by spicy oak.
#18161
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

9. McManis Family Syrah, River Junction - 2007 (750 mL)
Soft and fleshy, with ripe tannins, complex flavors of blackberry and cherry, and just a touch of cedar and smoke. This red screams for black pepper-crusted pork loin.
#10990
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

8. Dancing Bull Zinfandel - 2006 (750 mL)
Entry-level Zin for anyone who wants to get their feet wet. A bit lighter and friendlier than most, this Zinfandel nonetheless shows varietally typical flavors of bramble and dark fruits. Its relatively muted tannins (for a Zin, that is) make it an extremely versatile food wine.
#86322
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

7. Qupé Syrah, Central Coast - 2006 (750 mL)
A tasty and aromatic Syrah from Qupé, a specialist in Rhône grapes. This medium-bodied wine boasts classic Syrah flavors: black fruit and a touch of black pepper.
#87624
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

6. Nalle Zinfandel, Dry Creek Valley - 2006 (750 mL)
Lovely raspberry aromas and dry, earthy aromatics greet the nose, followed by a moderate dose of oak. This medium-bodied Zin shows good acidity and tannins that are supportive yet unobtrusive. It has succulent, intense strawberry fruit on the palate, and a finish that is a touch compressed at the moment. Lingering earth and light toast notes frame the brambly fruit. This is an exceptionally civilized California Zinfandel.
#61284
Original Price: $28.99
Sale Price: $24.64

5. Cline Zinfandel, California - 2006 (750 mL)
Fred Cline has restored some of California's oldest vineyards. Even his value-driven baseline bottling is bona fide old-vine Zinfandel. The age of the vines shows in the intense complexity of this excellent full-bodied red.
#62594
Original Price: $10.99
Sale Price: $9.34

4. Shannon Ridge Syrah - 2006 (750 mL)
This dark mahogany red has flavors of ripe blackberry and hints of brown sugar. Grilled meats would pair perfectly with this big, bold and strong Syrah.
#20875
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. Dashe "l'Enfant Terrible", Zinfandel - 2007 (750 mL)
What puts this Zin in a class all its own, apart from its low alcohol and solid tannic structure, is that it is fermented using natural yeasts, rather than commercial ones. This gives it all the character of its place of origin, the Alexander Valley. On the nose, it's full of sweet mixed red and black berries, but the palate is totally dry. A rare treat from the Zin category.
#20797
Original Price: $25.99
Sale Price: $22.09

2. Arcadian Syrah, Stolpman Vineyard - 2003 (750 mL)
These vines are just beginning to show what upper-end Syrah can do in California. A fantastic, spicy red wine showing endless blackberried fruit on the nose, with tar and freshly ground peppercorns.
#15924
Original Price: $61.99
Sale Price: $42.98

1. Cline Zinfandel Ancient Vines - 2007 (750 mL)
This mouth-coating Zinfandel offers flavors of toasted caramel and dark berries. Soft tannins and a lasting vanilla finish make the Ancient Vine Zin dangerously easy to drink.
#65011
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59
