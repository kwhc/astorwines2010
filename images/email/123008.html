Our Top 11
Austrian Wines
(...our lists go up to 11)


11. Zweigelt Classic, Iby - 2006 (750 mL)
Anton Iby only makes red wines in the Burganland of Austria. His Zweigelt Classic shows gorgeous red cherry fruit and soft tannins. As the name says, this is a very traditional example of a great Zweigelt.
#19500
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

10. Grüner Veltliner Federspiel "Stein Am Rain" Jamek - 2007 (750 mL)
Importer Terry Theise finds Jamek's wines to be more "authoritative" than purely brilliant. This one has expressive green aromatics and lots of dense minerality on the palate. The finish is deliciously herbal.
#20925
Original Price: $31.99
Sale Price: $27.19

9. Riesling "Loibenberg" Smaragd, Alzinger - 2005 (750 mL)
Alzinger is one of the top producers in the Wachau, making pure and focused Rieslings. The Loibenberg bottling is packed with mineral, with notes of honeysuckle that evoke the first days of spring.
#19724
Original Price: $49.99
Sale Price: $42.49

8. St. Laurent, Sattler - 2006 (750 mL)
Next time you want to bring a Merlot to a party, but then change your mind because you're worried your friends might harbor a prejudice against Merlot, take this pretty, soft red wine instead. Made from the St. Laurent grape, this has the velvety texture of a Merlot and it's a pleasure on its own or with food. Very impressive.
#20803
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

7. Gelber Muskateller, Ecker - 2007 (750 mL)
This Austrian Muscat is refreshingly zippy on the palate with great acidity and aromatics: it's redolent of nutmeg, elderflower, and lychee. Try this bottle out if you're in the mood for a dry Muscat.
#20922
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

6. Pinot Noir "Fuchsenfeld" Pittnauer - 2005 (750 mL)
The husband and wife behind this wine specialize in Pinot Noir and St. Laurent. Here they have created a very Burgundian example of the variety with a gamey, sauvage quality and a softness that brings to mind lighter Côtes de Beaune wines.
#20562
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

5. Weissburgunder, Heidi Schröck - 2007 (750 mL)
"The family motto states that tradition should be honored but also mixed with progress; for it means keeping alive the fire, not adoring the ashes," informs the distributor's website. It's an apt sentiment to describe the Schröck estate. This complex wine offers a range of flavors, from orange peel to scallop shell.
#20802
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

4. Grüner Veltliner "Obere Steigen," Huber - 2007 (750 mL)
Grüner Veltliner comes in many forms, and this is one of its rounder and more full-fruited manifestations. The wine is unoaked and dry, with a broad mid-palate full of white flowers and pears.
#20465
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

3. Vienna White Blend, Wieninger - 2005 (750 mL)
This crisp white, known as the "Vienna Blend," is a refreshing blend of Grüner Veltliner, Welschriesling, and Sauvignon Blanc. The perfect white for fish, it's light and crisp, and it goes down smooth.
#18127
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

2. Riesling "Gobelsburger", Schloss Gobelsburg - 2007 (750 mL)
With notes of green tea, verbena, pear, lime-blossom, and wisteria, this is an absolute masterpiece from the Schloss Gobelsburg estate. A gorgeous, structured, and focused Riesling.
#15553
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

1. Grüner Veltliner "Gobelsburger," Schloss Gobelsburg - 2007 (750 mL)
A bright, mineral-laden white from one of the top producers in Austria, this Grüner is packed with white pepper and ripe fruit on the palate.
