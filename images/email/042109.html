Our Top 11
Oregon Wines
(...our lists go to 11)


11. Pinot Noir "Cuvée A", Anne Amie - 2007
Cuvée A is a classic Oregon Pinot Noir. The bouquet is filled with aromas of spices, fresh black raspberries, and hints of toast. On the palate, you'll find cranberries and ripe black cherries, with accents of sage and cinnamon. The finish is lively and long, with a clean freshness that invites another sip. Excellent with seafood, such as salmon or halibut.
#18333
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

10. Argyle Pinot Noir, Willamette Valley - 2007
Rich and fruity Pinot Noir is the focus at this winery in the Willamette Valley in Oregon. Medium-bodied, soft berry fruit and simply great varietal character keeps us coming back for more.
#00122
Original Price: $28.99
Sale Price: $24.64

9. A to Z "Night & Day" Red - 2005
A hearty Oregon red that is 100% barrel-fermented for 18 months. This is an unusual blend of grape varieties from Bordeaux, Spain and the Rhône. Beautifully aromatic, with succulent flavors of plums, currants, boysenberries and cocoa. A powerful, fruit-forward wine, this is excellent when you're in the mood for something different.
#17950
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

8. Ponzi, Pinot Gris - 2007
A full-bodied, unoaked expression of Pinot Gris with round yellow plums, smoky aromas, and a floral finish. The texture is rich and can easily stand up to any number of fish or white meat dishes.
#20543
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

7. Maysara "Jamsheed" Pinot Noir - 2006
This rather luscious Pinot Noir from McMinnville, Oregon is a great example of the fruit-forward style of American Pinot Noir that so many have grown to love. On the nose, the wine gives plenty of floral berry character. Though heavy on the jammy fruit, the palate is balanced and soft, and supple tannins round out the equation.
#20563
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

6. Alloro Pinot Noir - 2006
Especially coming from such a young winery, this is an impressively advanced bottle of Pinot Noir. Its distinct aroma of musk makes it unlike any Oregon wine we've ever had. This is full of mixed red berry flavors and reminds us of a great Burgundy.
#18200
Original Price: $39.99
Sale Price: $33.99

5. A to Z Chardonnay - 2006
It's quite the success story: This Chardonnay came about through a partnership of the two families that founded A to Z in 2001 - and A to Z is now the fastest-growing winery in the Willamette Valley. Fermented and aged mainly in stainless steel, this white wine has a bright, crisp character.
#15789
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

4. Rock Point, Pinot Noir - 2008
This Oregon Pinot Noir is particularly Burgundian, which is probably why we love it so much! The nose is full of brambly, dried red fruits that carry through to the palate. This wine is all about delicacy and finesse.
#20963
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

3. Ransom "Junehog," Oregon - 2007
Fascinatingly aromatic yet admirably poised and restrained, this idiosyncratic blend of Müller-Thurgau, Pinot Blanc, Pinot Gris, and Riesling will startle you - but it'll do it quietly. Bits of the bouquet call to mind a nice Chardonnay (which seems to be the one white grape that isn't in this blend), yet there are innumerable exotic aromas and flavors flitting around a solid core of firm acidity. This is a must-try: aside from being delicious, it's completely unlike anything we've ever had.
#18648
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

2. Penner-Ash Viognier - 2006
A wonderful dry white wine with subtle fruit and floral flavors: citrus and pear take center stage, but a subtle minerality is present as well. The wine is unoaked and aged on the lees for 5 months before bottling.
#19436
Original Price: $59.99
Sale Price: $50.99

1. Stringtown, Pinot Noir - 2006
This luscious Pinot Noir comes from various plots of prime vineyards located throughout the beautiful Willamette Valley in Oregon. This red wine is full of raspberry and cherry fruit on the palate.
#17858
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99
