Astor's Top Provence Wines
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Bandol Rosé, Ch. de Pibarnon - 2008 (375 mL)
From one of the best estates in this appellation, famous for its rosé, comes this beautiful example of a classic style. Dry and full, yet lifted by acidity and profound salty minerality, this wine offers a beautiful array of fresh and dried berry notes.
#21642
Original Price: $15.99 (half-bottle)
Sale Price: $13.59

10. Cassis Blanc, Dom. du Bagnol - 2007
The sub-region of Cassis is located in Provence in the south of France. This is a sophisticated white wine made from Ugni Blanc, Clairette Blanc, and Roussane. The aromas are driven by pears and quince. The minerals are subtle at first but grow on the palate. Drink with a light chill.
#19413
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

9. Dom. Triennes Viognier, Ste. Fleur - 2007
A fresh, dry white wine with apricot and peach floral aromas and lovely minerals on the finish. This is a fruit-laden Viognier that you can enjoy tonight.
#15479
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

8. Bandol Rosé, Ch. Pradeaux - 2008
From stunning hillside vineyards overlooking the Mediterranean, comes a rich, flavorful Bandol rosé. With fragrant aromas of flowers and Provençal herbs, this dry wine is perfect for bouillabaisse or herb-flavored grilled meats.
#21014
Original Price: $25.99
Sale Price: $22.09

7. Mas de Gourgonnier, Baux de Provence - 2006
This robust red blend is a crowd favorite that is drinking exceedingly well in its youth. Chock-full of dark berries with a touch of anise, this wine gets a little support from an approachable tannic structure.
#15414
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

6. VdP du Mont-Caume, Jean Pierre Gaussen - 2006
A structured and dark-fruited blend made mostly of Mourvèdre, hailing from Bandol in Provence. Gaussen uses more Grenache than is allowed under the Bandol AOC rules, so this is classified as a Vin de Pays.
#21451
Original Price: $10.99
Sale Price: $9.34

5. Côtes de Provence Rosé "MiP", Dom. Sainte Lucie - 2008
A stunningly pale, floral-perfumed rosé that offers absolutely everything you could want in a dry pink wine: freshness, a near-weightless mouthfeel, and tangy berry flavors.
#21555
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

4. Côte de Provence Rosé, Domaine de la Sauveuse - 2008
A dry rosé that's just full of rose petals on the nose and strawberries on the palate. Full-bodied and structured, this southern French wine is naturally a great match for rich, meat-based dishes.
#20358
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

3. "Amourvèdre", Vin de Table, Comptoirs de Magdala - 2007
A stupendous Vin de Table from Antoine Pouponneau in Provence. Based on Mourvèdre, as the name might suggest.
#21303
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

2. Dom. du Deffends "Clos du Bécassier" Rouge - 2006
Domaine du Deffends is a traditional family estate located in the Coteaux Varois in Provence. The estate is well-known for its rosé but not to be forgotten is this delicious, ripe red wine that's dense with berries and an elegant spice.
#20537
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99
