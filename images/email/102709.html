Astor's Top Chilean Wines
...our lists go up to 11.


11. Casa Julia Sauvignon Blanc - 2008
A new offering from Chile. This is a gleaming, pale yellow wine with notes of green. It offers plenty of fruit, both tropical and citrus, with slight herbal notes. In the mouth it has a young, lively citrus taste, plus other tropical fruit flavors. Great body and a long finish.
#21795
Original Price: $7.99
Sale Price: $6.79

10. Kuyen, Alto del Maipo Valley - 2007
A dark blend of Syrah, Carmenère, and Cabernet Sauvignon with soft oak on the end.
#22297
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

9. Amayna, Sauvignon Blanc, Leyda Valley - 2008
Leyda, a sub-region of San Antonio, is located in a valley between the Coastal Mountain Range and the Pacific Ocean, running parallel to Chile's Central Valley. This wine has lots of tropical fruit, dominated by white pineapple and white flowers. A dry, distinctively New World Sauvignon Blanc.
#19470
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

8. Amayna, Pinot Noir - 2006
Pinot Noir in Chile has come a long way since it was first planted there, and we were extremely impressed with the Amayna bottling. Taste this wine blind and there can be no doubt that this is pure, delicious Pinot Noir (albeit a bit heftier in style than most). It's full of wild raspberries, dark cherries, and spice.
#19478
Original Price: $28.99
Sale Price: $24.64

7. Echeverria Cabernet Sauvignon - 2007
From the Curico Valley of Chile comes this rich Cabernet Sauvignon. Pretty blackberry and red cherry fruits greet the nose, and there is a touch of mint on the finish.
#12218
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

6. Casa Lapostolle "Clos Apalta" - 2006
Here is an award-winning Bordeaux blend based on the Carmenère variety, which used to be widely planted in Bordeaux and now thrives in Chile. The wine is made from the estate's oldest vines, the berries picked and de-stemmed by hand for the highest possible quality. A big and age-worthy red wine.
#21038
Original Price: $89.99
Sale Price: $76.49

5. Chono Reserva Carménère - 2006
This is a hearty, dark red wine made from the Carmenère grape variety. It's aged in oak for a short period of time to give it just a little spice on the palate. An excellent choice for anything barbecued or meat-based.
#17923
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

4. Concha y Toro and Rothschild, Almaviva - 2006
This Bordeaux-style blend of Cabernet Sauvignon, Carmenère, and Cabernet Franc is built like a Pauillac: dense yet elegant, with raspberry ganache, blackcurrant, and fig fruit layered with loam, tobacco, mineral, and cedar notes. Impressive balance among tannins, acidity, and oak.
#54629
Original Price: $89.99
Sale Price: $76.49

3. "Neyen" Espiritu de Apalta, Colchagua - 2004
Neyen means "spirit" in the native Mapudungun language. The Neyen vineyard was the first in Apalta (Colchagua valley); these Carmenère and Cabernet Sauvignon vines were planted in 1890. This red wine is a full-bodied and full of spiced cassis and blackberry fruit.
#19484
Original Price: $56.99
Sale Price: $48.44

2. Cousiño Macul Finis Terrae, Maipo - 2007
This red blend is deep, dark, and complex. Blackberries, violets, and cocoa abound in the bouquet, followed on the palate by cherries, rosemary, and black truffle. This has a lengthy, spicy finish with subtle tannins.
#54504
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

1. Echeverria Unwooded Chardonnay - 2008
A Chardonnay unlike any other in the New World. This is a hand-crafted wine without any trace of oak influence. Aromas of tropical fruits and pears intermingle with mouthwatering acidity, while the wine retains a lovely round feel on the palate.
#12611
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49
