our top 11:
Australian Wines
(... we go to eleven)


11. Long Flat Yarra Valley "Destinations", Pinot Noir - 2006
Straightforward, compote-y, and nicely spiced, this is a modern, approachable expression of Pinot Noir.
#15186
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

10. Shaw and Smith, "M3" Vineyard Chardonnay - 2006
Shaw and Smith's overarching goal is to prove that Australia can grow grapes that are typically grown in cooler climates. This Chardonnay is impressive in every sense. Full of white floral fruits, it is reminiscent of a southern French white Burgundy, made with hand-picked fruit aged in neutral French oak.
#20063
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

9. Grant Burge Riesling, Thorn Family Vineyard - 2007
Grant Burge is a fifth-generation winemaker located in the Barossa Valley. This Thorn Ridge Riesling comes from Barossa's Eden Valley, which is a cooler microclimate known for producing crisp whites. This dry white wine has a leesy nose, with fresh lime and underlying sage on the palate. A perfect match for oysters.
#15047
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

8. Holy Trinity, Grant Burge - 2002
This hearty, Rhône-like, red wine is named after an Anglican church of the area. It's spicy, packed with mixed dark berries and vanilla. An excellent match to any meat-based cuisine - especially grilled sausages.
#74035
Original Price: $37.99
Sale Price: $32.29

7. Over the Shoulder, Sauvignon Blanc - 2006
We were blown away by the fresh fruit flavors of this Sauvignon Blanc. In fact, the bright citrus and herbaceous fruit characteristics had us completely fooled: we never would have guessed that this was from Australia.
#18016
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

6. Avignon, Kaesler - 2005 (750)
This savory red wine is medium-bodied by Australian standards. It spends 12 months in older oak, with the Grenache contributing soft fruit aromas, the Shiraz adding peppery spice, and the Mourvèdre giving a nice edge to the tannins.
#19441
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

5. Tamar Ridge "Devil's Corner" Pinot Noir - 2007
How often do you see a Pinot Noir from Tasmania? This bottle represents an opportunity that may not come around again for a while. It is an easy-to-drink red, full of soft, spicy berry fruit flavors
#18202
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

4. Burge Family Garnacha, Barossa Valley - 2005
A full-bodied red wine with dark berries - blackberries, currants and blueberries - with a touch of mint and spice on the finish. Moderate tannins help to balance out the fruit character. Made from mostly 80-year-old vines that have been dry farmed. 80 years ago, Garnacha wasn't as popular as the "port-like" red wines of those days. Burge didn't follow trends, however, and the estate has those ancient Garnacha vines to this day. Today, Rick Burge propagates his vineyard from old-vine cuttings.
#13271
Original Price: $44.99
Sale Price: $38.24

3. Yering Station Pinot Noir - 2006
With aromas of herbs, cherries, raspberries, and warm spices on the palate, this medium-bodied wine is quite stunning. Open a bottle tonight and you’ll immediately understand why Yering Station has received tremendous accolades in Australia this past year.
#53415
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

2. Black Chook Shiraz Viognier - 2007
McLaren Vale has an unrivaled reputation in Australia, and indeed the world, for growing full-flavored Shiraz that is ripe yet balanced. This one makes use of a technique perfected in France's Côte-Rôtie: the winemakers add a little Viognier to the mix, creating a stunning and complex wine.
#19312
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

1. Kay Brothers "Amery Vineyards" Hillside Shiraz - 2004
Kay Brothers is one of the oldest wineries in McLaren Vale, and over the years they've truly perfected their craft. This is a smoky, full-bodied red wine that's full of dark, fleshy berried fruit and eucalyptus.
#14284
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49
