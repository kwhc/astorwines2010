our top 11:
Wines of Australia
(... we go to eleven)

11. Over the Shoulder, Sauvignon Blanc (2006)
We were blown away by the fresh fruit flavors of this Sauvignon Blanc. In fact, the bright citrus and herbaceous fruit characteristics had us completely fooled: we never would have guessed that this was from Australia.
#18016
Sale Price: $12.74

10. d'Arenberg Shiraz, Footbolt (2004)
Rich, full-flavored Shiraz sourced from late-19th-century vines in the heart of South Australia's McLaren Vale. Marvelous texture with plum, blackcurrant, and blueberry flavors paving the way to a long, rewarding finish.
#53094
Sale Price: $15.29

9. Tamar Ridge "Devil's Corner" Pinot Noir (2005)
How often do you see a Pinot Noir from Tasmania? This bottle represents an opportunity that may not come around again for a while. It is an easy-to-drink red, full of soft, spicy berry fruit flavors.
#18202
Sale Price: $13.59

8. Evans & Tate Chardonnay, Margaret River (2006)
A portion of this wine is fermented in barrique to add texture and a touch of warm flavors - vanilla in particular. Malolactic fermentation is avoided in order to maintain the wine's firm, fresh acidity. Full of tropical fruit and ripe peaches, with excellent minerality and complexity on the palate.
#17947
Sale Price: $16.14

7. Grant Burge Riesling, Thorn Family Vineyard (2007)
Grant Burge is a fifth-generation winemaker located in the Barossa Valley. This Thorn Ridge Riesling comes from Barossa's Eden Valley, which is a cooler microclimate known for producing crisp whites. This dry white wine has a leesy nose, with fresh lime and underlying sage on the palate. A perfect match for oysters.
#15047
Sale Price: $16.99

6. Loan, Semillon (2005)
Australian white wines have to be made with particular diligence in order to maintain good levels of refreshing acidity. Dry-farmed and aged in neutral French oak, this white has been well taken care of, and tastes terrific before, during, and after dinner. Incredibly ripe with pear and quince fruit aromas.
#20062
Sale Price: $17.84

5. Kangarilla Cabernet Sauvignon (2006)
Lush with juicy, dark berries and a touch of mint on the palate. This full-bodied red is all about fruit and appoachability; it's easy to drink alone, and great with steak.
#13359
Sale Price: $17.84

4. Kilikanoon "Prodigal" Grenache (2005)
This is a brawny, full-bodied red wine that's full of blackberries, blueberries, and chocolate on the palate. Full of concentration and a great pick for those who like bold wines, this would be great with steak and fries.
#19271
Sale Price: $23.79

3. Tapestry "Bakers Gully" Shiraz (2006)
Tapestry wines are unorthodox in many ways. Most importantly, it's not all about fruit, fruit, fruit with them: while this wine has lots of dark berries and plums on the palate, there are also subtle minerals, cedar, and hints of spice.
#19979
Sale Price: $13.59

2. Avignon, Kaesler (2005)
This savory red wine is medium-bodied by Australian standards. It spends 12 months in older oak, with the Grenache contributing soft fruit aromas, the Shiraz adding peppery spice, and the Mourvèdre giving a nice edge to the tannins.
#19441
Sale Price: $21.24

1. Shaw and Smith, "M3" Vineyard Chardonnay (2006)
Shaw and Smith's overarching goal is to prove that Australia can grow grapes that are typically grown in cooler climates. This Chardonnay is impressive in every sense. Full of white floral fruits, it is reminiscent of a southern French white Burgundy, made with hand-picked fruit that is aged in neutral French oak.
#20063
Sale Price: $25.49
