Astor's Top 11 Sicilian Wines
...Our lists go up to 11.


11. Cerasuolo di Vittoria, Gulfi - 2008
A delightful red wine that's full of raspberry and currant fruit aromas. Delicate, dry, and incredibly fresh and playful.
#22246
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

10. Faro, Palari - 2004
A blend of fascinating and largely unheard of indigenous red grapes: mostly Nerello Mascalese and Nerello Cappuccio, with small amounts of Nocera, Calabrese, Acitana, and more. Simply a beautiful red wine, with flavors of red berries, fresh tobacco, spices, and minerals. Changes by the minute.
#12039
Original Price: $74.99
Sale Price: $63.74

9. Nero Bufaleffi, Gulfi - 2000
This is made from the Sicilian red grape Nero d'Avola. It comes from a limestone-based vineyard around Pachino in the Val di Noto region of Sicily. This is a full-bodied, chocolatey red wine with a complex mix of black berries, vanilla, and mineral-driven tannins. It is always a favorite wine at the dinner table. This unfiltered bottling should be decanted to aerate the wine and separate the sediment.
#10711
Original Price: $37.99
Sale Price: $32.29

8. Salina Bianco, Hauner - 2008
A dry Sicilian white wine that's full of soft yellow fruits, laurel leaf, and honey. Made from a blend of Catarratto and Inzolia, two indigenous white grapes that thrive on the mostly volcanic soils of the island.
#19003
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

7. Grillo "Parlante," Fondo Antico - 2008
This white wine is pure fruit flavor on the palate - mostly pineapples and oranges - with a ripe and juicy appeal. Grillo is a white Sicilian grape variety best known for its robust contribution to Marsala (but don't worry: this bottle is completely dry).
#13963
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

6. Nero d'Avola, Feudo Montoni - 2005
A Sicilian Nero d'Avola that shows pure red-berried fruit, fresh acidity, and a touch of mineral on the finish. This red could easily be held for years, but it needn't be; if you're drinking it today, it will be excellent after a quick decanting.
#15815
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

5. Outis (Nessuno), Biondi - 2005
A beautiful red wine from Mount Etna in Sicily, made from a blend of the native Nerello Mascalese and Nerello Cappuccio grape varieties. On the palate you'll find fascinating, delicate notes of tobacco, red currants, red pepper, and anise.
#12225
Original Price: $25.99
Sale Price: $22.09

4. Rosso del Soprano, Palari - 2004
The fruit on this great red wine is a combination of delicate red berries, iron, powder, and sweet spice. This is made from a wild blend of indigenous grape varieties, grown at 1400 feet above sea level in northeastern Sicily.
#12962
Original Price: $32.99
Sale Price: $28.04

3. Nero Màccari, Gulfi - 2003
Nero d'Avola sourced from the Màccari vineyard, where it develops high acidity in the sunny Sicilian climes. This rich red wine has notes of blackberries, blueberries, eucalyptus, and tobacco.
#44442
Original Price: $43.99
Sale Price: $37.39

2. Vinupetra Rosso IGT, I Vigneri di Salvo Foti - 2005
Leave it to the expert, Salvo Foti, to get his hands on old vines of red indigenous varieties planted on the volcanic Mount Etna. Foti is Sicily's most learned winemaker, and his passion and meticulous nature shine through in this unadulterated red. Decant and enjoy this today or hold it for years to come.
#16028
Original Price: $49.99
Sale Price: $42.49

1. Carricante/Carjcanti, Gulfi - 2006
Carricante, a white variety native to Mount Etna, also thrives in the Monte Iblei area of southeastern Sicily. Though truly a wine of finesse, this has a strong and robust structure, with notes of grapefruit, almonds, and chalky minerality on the palate. Decant and enjoy this today or hold it for up to a decade. From a Practicing Organic winemaker.
#12109
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69
