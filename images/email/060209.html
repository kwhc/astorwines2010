Astor's Top Australian Wines
(our lists go up to 11)


11. Three Rings Shiraz - 2007
Made from a trio of fine winemakers in the Barossa Valley of Australia. This speaks Barossa in the sense that it's bold, incredibly ripe and compote-fruit-like and it's spicy and full of coffee tannins on the finish.
#18190
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

10. Amaroo Shiraz - 2007
With blackberry fruit and a spicy finish, this Shiraz will go with everything from a summer barbecue to lighter fare. It's friendly enough to please a crowd, yet complex enough to reward repeated tastings; try it alongside a few different cuisines to see how it changes with food.
#12235
Original Price: $11.99
Sale Price: $10.19

9. Jacobs Creek Chardonnay - 2007
Big, round Barossa character on a modern-styled Aussie white.
#53074
Original Price: $6.99
Sale Price: $5.94

8. Heartland Cabernet Sauvignon - 2006
From the Langhorne Creek, this Cabernet offers powdery notes and smashed granite mineral on the nose. The wine is full of dark fruit notes mingling nicely with hints of spice from 12 months in oak. Rich, ripe, and full bodied.
#21161
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

7. Yering Station Pinot Noir - 2006
With aromas of herbs, cherries, raspberries, and warm spices on the palate, this medium-bodied wine is quite stunning. Open a bottle tonight and you’ll immediately understand why Yering Station has received tremendous accolades in Australia this past year.
#53415
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

6. Burge Family Garnacha, Barossa Valley - 2005
A full-bodied red wine with dark berries - blackberries, currants and blueberries - with a touch of mint and spice on the finish. Moderate tannins help to balance out the fruit character. Made from mostly 80-year-old vines that have been dry farmed. 80 years ago, Garnacha wasn't as popular as the "port-like" red wines of those days. Burge didn't follow trends, however, and the estate has those ancient Garnacha vines to this day. Today, Rick Burge propagates his vineyard from old-vine cuttings.
#13271
Original Price: $44.99
Sale Price: $38.24

5. Avignon, Kaesler - 2005
This savory red wine is medium-bodied by Australian standards. It spends 12 months in older oak, with the Grenache contributing soft fruit aromas, the Shiraz adding peppery spice, and the Mourvèdre giving a nice edge to the tannins.
#19441
Original Price: $24.99
Sale Price: $21.24

4. Paxton AAA, Shiraz Grenache - 2006
This blend of 75% Shiraz and 25% Grenache is also grown biodynamically. Rich with spicy character, but not over the top thanks to the softening effect of the Grenache.
#20915
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. Kay Brothers "Amery Vineyards" Hillside Shiraz - 2004
Kay Brothers is one of the oldest wineries in McLaren Vale, and over the years they've truly perfected their craft. This is a smoky, full-bodied red wine that's full of dark, fleshy berried fruit and eucalyptus. Far more subtle than what you might expect from an Australian Shiraz.
#14284
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

2. d'Arenberg Shiraz, Footbolt - 2005
Rich, full-flavored Shiraz sourced from late-19th-century vines in the heart of South Australia's McLaren Vale. Marvelous texture with plum, blackcurrant, and blueberry flavors paving the way to a long, rewarding finish.
#53094
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.99

1. Richard Hamilton Viognier - 2006
This Viognier is aged for a short period of time in oak, which adds a touch of body and complexity. The wood never obscures the fruit in this dry white wine, however, and it remains very clean and pure on the palate. With aromas of pineapple, white flowers and mixed peaches, this is an elegant apéritif wine, or an excellent match for light meat dishes and seafood.
#16914
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14
