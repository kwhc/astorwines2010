Astor's Top Wines from Argentina
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Porteño, Mendoza - 2007
With its ruby red color and aromas of black cherries and plums, this Argentine red is simply one of the best wines to come out of Mendoza in years. The tannins are silky-smooth and work perfectly with grilled meats or hard, aged cheeses.
#13492
Original Price: $4.99
Sale Price: $4.24

10. Mapema, Sauvignon Blanc - 2008
Another fantastic easy-drinking white from Argentina, this Sauvignon Blanc is dry, fresh, floral, and free of any oak influence.
#19901
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

9. Conquista, Malbec - 2007
This red wine from Mendoza hits all the right notes. An aromatic cherry nose and big round tannins make this a great entry-level Malbec.
#21466
Original Price: $8.99
Sale Price: $7.64

8. Paso de Piedra Cabernet, Viña Alicia - 2006
A full-bodied, modern Cabernet Sauvignon full of ripe, dark fruit, and nuanced oak notes. The nose offers notes of currant, blackberry, and spice while the palate is savory and meaty.
#21701
Original Price: $20.99
Sale Price: $15.99

7. Punto Final Malbec Reserva - 2006
This rich red is aged in French oak for 16 months and then in bottle for a further 6 months. It is deep violet-red in color, with a nose that reveals a combination of red fruits and toasty notes from the oak. On the palate it is a complex and structured wine with a long finish.
#16396
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

6. Jelu Syrah - 2006
The Jelu winery, located in the San Juan region of Argentina, is located at an elevation of 900 feet. "Jelu" means sun in the native language, and these grapes do in fact achieve maximum ripeness from all the hot Argentine sun they get. Blackberry fruit, pepper, and great underlying minerality make this a tasty and crowd-pleasing wine.
#14889
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $8.49

5. Piazzolla Bonarda, Mendoza - 2008
The red Bonarda grape variety is widely grown in Argentina. This is a hearty red wine, free of oak, that is full of mulberry fruit aromas with a touch of graphite and thyme on the finish. It goes very well with steak and grilled meats.
#12805
Original Price: $6.99
Sale Price: $5.94

4. Familia Mayol Bonarda - 2007
Bonarda is one of the most popular and successful red grapes grown in Argentina. This fantastic wine is from the Mendoza region and gives us everything we want in a full-bodied red: spice, lush black fruit (blueberries and blackberries), and medium grip from the tannins.
#14421
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

3. Torrontés "Plata," La Rioja - 2008
This brilliant white wine is crafted in northern Argentina’s La Rioja region. The Torrontés grape, a distant cousin of Muscat, produces distinctively aromatic wines, and we think they are some of the best bottles to come out of Argentina lately. The “Plata” is dry on the palate, with notes of mixed white flowers, yellow plums, and even some white peppercorn on the finish. Overall, it has a remarkably fresh character, enabling it to complement lighter dishes without overwhelming them. It’s fresh and surprising each and every time you open a bottle.
#17296
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $7.99

2. Benmarco Malbec - 2008
Susana Balbo makes this supple and full-bodied red from 50-year-old vines. The age of the vines gives this wine uncommon richness and concentration, making it an extraordinarily sophisticated Malbec.
#75487
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

1. Rosé of Malbec, Cristobal 1492 - 2008
The Malbec grape was once popular in Bordeaux, but these days it is mostly associated with Argentina (the Mendoza region in particular). Here we have a rosé of Malbec from Mendoza: it is fully dry, yet bursting with red berry fruits. With a chill, it is as crisp and delicious as any rosé we've tasted.
#20369
Original Price: $9.99
Sale Price: $6.99
