our top 11:
Wines of Germany
(... we go to eleven)


11. Ürziger Würzgarten Riesling Eiswein, Christoffel (2004)
From the tiny Christoffel estate in the Mosel, this is a complex Eiswein with huge aging potential.
#16731
Original Price: $99.99
Sale Price: $84.99

10. Nahe Riesling "Monziger Halenberg" Spätlese, Schäfer-Fröhlich (2006)
A plump Riesling with great balance and yellow stone fruit aromas: yellow plums, yellow peaches, and a touch of Meyer lemon. Finishes more off-dry than bone-dry. Drink chilled.
#19971
Original Price: $37.99
Sale Price: $32.29

9. Riesling Kabinett "Jean-Baptiste", Gunderloch (2007)
Fritz Hasselbach, owner of Weingut Gunderloch, is not only one of the premier producers of the Rheinhessen, he's also incredibly
dedicated to making wines of terroir. The red, iron-rich soils show through on the palate. Off-dry on the palate, full of peaches and yellow
plums.
#14110
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

8. Erdener Treppchen Riesling Kabinett, Christoffel (2006)
A kabinett that's pushing the kabinett-level delicacy making it all the more potent. Full of peaches, powder and dense minerals. Nicely
round and sweet making it a wine to enjoy in its youth or in a decade.
#41131
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39

7. Dürkheimer Fronhof Scheurebe Kabinett Halbtrocken, Dart (2006)
Kurt Darting has always had a knack for knowing what his customers want. This white wine is aromatic, full of grapefruit and flower
character, and has just a hint of sweetness on the palate. It's incredibly versatile and pairs well with all kinds of assertively spiced foods.
#20146
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

6. Gleisweiler Hölle Riesling Spätlese, Minges (2006)
Without doubt, Theo Minges is making some of the zippiest, most minerally Rieslings outside of the Mosel! Dry with tangy kiwi-lime
fruit, razor sharp acidity and an intensely slatey finish, enjoy this with light but flavorful fish dishes.
#50236
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

5. Dorsheimer Goldloch Riesling Spätlese, Schlossgut Diel (1989)
One of the greatest estates on the lower Nahe, producing scintillating, terroir-driven Rieslings. This one is just entering its prime.
#19213
Original Price: $64.99
Sale Price: $55.24

4. Zelt Himmel Ries Auslese, Selbach-Oster (2006)
The vineyards of the middle Mosel owe their international acclaim to a combination of slate soil, southern exposure, and death-defying
steepness. With steely minerality and a whiff of botrytis, this offering from Selbach-Oster delivers a mouthful of orchard fruit balanced by
a healthy dose of acidity.
#20267
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

3. Graacher Domprobst Riesling BA, Schaeffer (2005)
Despite owning a mere 6 acres of vines, Willi Schaefer manages to produce the best Rieslings from the famous vineyards of Graach. This
unctuous and decadent Riesling is made from individually picked grapes and packed with richness.
#89005
Original Price: $124.99
Sale Price: $106.24

2. Haardter Bürgergarten Riesling Auslese, Müller-Catoir (2004)
Despite the high level of sugar in this Auslese, the balanced acidity comes through, giving this wine a tart lemon notes. Perfect botrytis
and structure.
#16732
Original Price: $39.99
Sale Price: $33.99

1. Riesling "Unplugged," Tesch (2006)
This Kabinett Trocken comes from several vineyard sites in the Nahe that have been in the family since the 1700s. It shows very focused and concentrated fruit on the palate, yet is thoroughly dry and full of minerals. Look for juicy notes of Meyer lemon (and then try and put your glass down).
#16678
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59
