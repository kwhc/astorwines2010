our top 11:
Sakés
(... we go to eleven)

11. Otokoyama Saké, Junmai
One of the most popular sakés, this very dry (SMV +10) bottle from Hokkaido features grain-based flavors and vivid acidity. This full-bodied saké is a traditional favorite with izakaya fare and beef-based dishes such as yaki-niku.
#59694
Sale Price: $22.94

10. Poochi-Poochi Saké
Slightly sparkling, off-dry, and only lightly filtered, this saké shows delightful, creamy notes of rice, buttermilk, and cantaloupe. Gently turn the bottle upside down once before opening. Enjoy chilled as an apéritif.
#11896
Sale Price: $11.04

9. Kanbara "Wings of Fortune" Saké, Junmai Ginjo
This is a Genshu saké, and therefore richer than you might expect since it has not been diluted with water (as most sakés are). Vibrant, concentrated, and spritzy, it is full of round raw coconut and honeydew flavors.
#18195
Sale Price: $33.99

8. Ohyama Saké, Tokubetsu Junmai
Ohyama makes a very well-structured saké, fully showcasing the distinctive yeast indigenous to Yamagata prefecture. This full-bodied saké starts sweet before unleashing its additional layers of complexity, leading to a medium-dry finish. Serve chilled or warm.
#59684
Sale Price: $22.94

7. Akitabare Koshiki Juntsukuri Saké, Junmai
Aromas of white flowers and citrus. Rich with a sharp rice character to it. A versatile saké that can be enjoyed with either a light chill or light warming.
#23112
Sale Price: $8.49

6. Sato No Homare Saké, Junmai Ginjo
A dry and delightful saké with firm flavors of green melon, citrus, and apples.
#17363
Sale Price: $25.49

5. Dassai Migaki "Niwari Sanbu" Saké, Junmai Daiginjo
Niwari-Sanbu means "23%," meaning that each kernel of rice was milled down to 23% of its original size - resulting in a subtle, delicate brew that can please the most demanding saképhile. It is lithe in texture with aromas and flavors of honeydew melon and McIntosh apples.
#16762
Sale Price: $62.89

4. Dewazakura Saké, Daiginjo
Rich and full of pineapple fruit aromas on the palate, this drinks well as an apéritif or alongside rich, meaty fish dishes like halibut. The bottle sports a detail that is quite rare for a saké: the brewer has chosen to list its vintage, indicating when the rice was harvested.
#14114
Sale Price: $14.44

3. Ryusei Tokubetsu Saké, Junmai
Simply beautiful on the nose and palate, this saké never disappoints with its soft, intriguing aromas of honeydew, cantaloupe, and orange peels. It is fruity and approaching off-dry on the palate. Enjoy it lightly chilled.
#14163
Sale Price: $30.59

2. Daishichi Kimoto Classic Saké, Junmai
"Kimoto" refers to the yeast starter used to ferment this saké. Some argue that this original method used to ferment the brew results in a deep, rich saké with more ripe flavors than acidity - and we tend to agree with them.
#15239
Sale Price: $30.59

1. Koshinohomare "Shiboritate" Saké, Junmai Nama Genshu
This seasonal saké is released every spring. It's an unpasteurized Genshu (the latter term means that it's not diluted with water, as most sakés are). This year's brew is flat-out delicious - strongly reminiscent of fresh cream and citrus fruits on the palate. If you tasted this blind, you might guess it was a Nigori: it's just that creamy in texture. Well worth a try, whether you're a beginner or a lifelong saké drinker.
#13133
Sale Price: $23.79
