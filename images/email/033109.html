Our Top 11
California White Wines
(...our lists go to 11)


11. Norton Ridge Chardonnay, Napa - 2006
Bottled by Napa's Joe Briggs, this is a clean, unoaked Chardonnay with plenty of bright citrus flavors and round honey notes. This definitely won't weigh you down during a long meal or a party; it is made to be refreshing and drinkable, with crisp acidity and a long finish.
#78007
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

10. Franciscan Chardonnay, Oakville - 2006
Quintessential California Chardonnay. A touch of oak, great fruit, and a clean finish. This full-bodied wine hits the spot after another exhausting New York day!
#82024
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

9. Mauritson Sauvignon Blanc - 2006
This Sauvignon Blanc is a blend of three vineyard sites in the Dry Creek Valley. Aromas of dried peach and freshly cut hay waft from the glass. Ripe fruit is balanced by a bright acidity, with flavors of grapefruit and honeysuckle coming forth on the palate.
#18742
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

8. Manifesto, Sauvignon Blanc - 2007
The Suisun Valley east of Carneros is perfect for Sauvignon Blanc, which benefits enormously from the region's diurnal temperature difference. The wine has a slightly grassy and very aromatic nose, which gives way to a clean palate that is devoid of oak and has lots of bright, mouth-watering acidity.
#20560
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

7. MacMurray Ranch Pinot Gris - 2007
Coming from California's Sonoma Coast, MacMurray ranch has created a delicious Pinot Gris that is fruit-forward with notes of fig and white peach, and a lingering finish. Pair this with scallops or lobster tonight.
#18407
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

6. Kenneth Volk Malvasia - 2006
This Malvasia comes from Monterey. Because of its rarity, Kenneth Volk considers this one of his heirloom varieties. The wine shows great acidity, with bright floral notes along with hints of citrus and melon. Delicious!
#19869
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

5. Four Vines Naked Chardonnay - 2007
This wine sees no oak, allowing the apple and citrus flavors to shine through. A California Chardonnay with good acidity can be hard to find, but this bottle from Santa Barbara is in a class all by itself. This is the quintessential food wine.
#20507
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

4. R. Mondavi Chardonnay Carneros - 2006
One of the most popular California Chardonnays, this wine from Robert Mondavi is versatile and extremely easy to drink. A lush, buttery texture supports pure, up-front fruit flavors.
#64384
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. Chalk Hill Sauvignon Blanc, Chalk Hill - 2006
A rich, round and well-oaked style of Sauvignon Blanc. Aromas of tropical and stone fruits enhance the minerality that shines through from the soil on which it is grown.
#65224
Original Price: $29.99
Sale Price: $25.49

2. Dashe Riesling - 2007
Charming and unorthodox winemaker Michael Dashe sources the grapes that go into this Riesling from the same excellent vineyard that brings us his magical Zinfandel, L'Enfant Terrible. On the nose, you'll get peaches and pineapples. The palate is balanced and harmonious. Who knew Cali Riesling could drink like this?!
#21497
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

1. Lioco Chardonnay, Sonoma County - 2007
Lioco has created this Chardonnay from a blend of different sources, with all fruit grown in dried creek beds throughout the Russian River. The wine is left in contact with the lees for six months, it does not see any oak, and it is bottled unfined and unfiltered. Fresh citrus notes play against a creamy mouthfeel.
#18154
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29
