Astor's Top Loire Valley Wines
...Our lists go to 11.


11. Cour-Cheverny, Dom. de Montcy - 2007
Cour-Cheverny is an appellation that was invented in order to protect the native Romorantin grape variety. This bottling is delicious and approachable, with stone fruit and raw honey aromas, bright acidity, and rocky minerality.
#21752
Original Price: $16.99
Sale Price: $14.44

10. Menetou-Salon Rouge, Gilbert - 2006
From the Menetou-Salon appellation, located just west of Sancerre, comes a Pinot Noir that is full of pretty, floral, and supple red berry fruit. There's a cool kiss of mineral on the finish, giving this a fuller feel on the palate.
#17796
Original Price: $22.99
Sale Price: $19.54

9. Anjou Blanc, Ch. Soucherie - 2008
From a producer known for exquisite Savennières comes this introductory-level Chenin Blanc. Notes of fresh peach, honeydew, and white flower are balanced by a crisp acidity.
#16080
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

8. Touraine Sauvignon, Clos Roche Blanche - 2008
From one of the best hillside sites in the Touraine, on chalky limestone and silex, comes this deceptively easygoing Sauvignon Blanc. It is light on the palate with very fresh fruit flavors, but underneath lies a long, deep mineral structure.
#23124
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

7. Anjou "Pur Breton," Cousin-Leduc - 2006
"Breton" is the local name for Cabernet Franc, and this one is a complex rendition, full of pomegranate fruit with a touch of leather and spice. Bacchus wholeheartedly approves, and so do we! Don't miss out on this juicy Loire Valley red.
#12851
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14

6. Anjou Rouge "Les Copines Aussi", Sablonettes - 2007
A light and fruity red wine made from the Gamay grape by a natural winemaker. It's light-bodied with red cherry and currant fruit on the palate.
#11415
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

5. Sancerre, Dom. Buissonnes - 2007
This white wine is from a family-run estate located in the village of Sury en Vaux. The wine rested on the lees until the April after the vintage. This vital process adds complexities that cannot be duplicated by other means. A great style of smoky Sancerre that's mineral-driven, floral, and bone dry on the finish, making it a marvelous match with fresh oysters.
#20688
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

4. Quincy "Très Vieilles Vignes", Dom. Mardon - 2008
It's amazing how differently Sauvignon Blanc can express itself in different terroirs. This version comes from the lesser-known Loire region of Quincy. It is remarkably aromatic - reminiscent of a Muscat - and full of white pineapple and citrus fruits on the palate. This dry, unoaked white is made (as the name says) from very old vines.
#26814
Original Price: $17.99
Sale Price: $15.29

3. La Bohème, Marc Pesnot - 2007
When Marc Pesnot refers to his young vines, he means they're "only" 30-50 years old! Needless to say, this young-vine cuvée is quite dense with ripe fruit, balanced out by bracing acidity and great minerality. The nose has seashell hints (typical of eastern Loire Valley soil) mingled with citrus notes. Truly a fresh and lovely offering.
#21146
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

2. Vouvray "Le Haut Lieu" Sec, Huet - 2006
Just recently released, this dry Vouvray is as fresh as they come. Honey, apricots, and mineral abound on the palate. Balanced and showing impressive finesse, this will settle in bottle nicely for years.
#19087
Original Price: $34.99
Sale Price: $29.74

1. Sancerre "Mélodie," Gaudry - 2006
Take a sip of Gaudry's brilliant Sancerre and you'll immediately taste the impact that 50-year-old vines can have on a wine. This is well concentrated, showing white fruit, fresh citrus, and floral aromas. Quite simply a gorgeous bottle of wine, and an excellent example of what old vines can do in the right hands.
#17694
Original Price: $23.99
Sale Price: $20.39
