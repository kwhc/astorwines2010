our top 11:
Beaujolais Wines
(... our lists go up to 11)


11. Beaujolais-Villages VV, Ch. du Chatelard - 2007 (750 mL)
This old-vine Beaujolais shows bright red fruit with underlying notes of clay and limestone minerality. Flavors of wild blackberries and currants abound, with a hint of earth just beneath the surface. Bright, lively acidity makes this an excellent food wine.
#19957
Original Price: $12.99
Sale Price: $11.04

10. Chiroubles, C. Pacalet - 2006 (750 mL)
A wine of character with distinct terroir. It has an earthy edge, but it’s the fruit that shines here: ripe raspberries, orange rind, cherry, and a touch of musk. Very fresh and very pretty, yet maintaining a mien of rusticity, the wine is produced organically, with no sulphur added.
#11953
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

9. Beaujolais Blanc, Ch. du Chatelard - 2007 (750 mL)
No, your eyes aren't deceiving you: this is indeed a white Beaujolais. Chardonnay is by far in the minority when it comes to wine production in this region. This one is unoaked, ripe, and pleasantly round in texture. It has also gained incredible concentration from the impressive age of the vines. Give it a try tonight!
#19462
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

8. Morgon VV, Daniel Bouland - 2007 (750 mL)
One of the 10 crus of the Beaujolais region, Morgon is noted for producing wines with darker, more powerful fruit profiles than are typical of the Gamay grape. Excellent character with a rustic, earthy edge that pairs well with the charcuterie (sausages, hams, country pâtés) so widely enjoyed in the region – not to mention here in NYC.
#14222
Original Price: $19.99
Sale Price: $16.99

7. Beaujolais "Pierre Chermette", Vissoux - 2007 (750 mL)
Brimming with fresh, bright, red raspberry fruit, this is a thirst-quenching wine of purity and finesse. It shines even among the scores of lively Beaujolais bottlings on the market today.
#21118
Original Price: $14.99
Sale Price: $12.74

6. Beaujolais Chardonnay, Terres Dorées, Brun - 2007 (750 mL)
Fruit, fat, and finesse, not to mention a solid mineral structure, garner rave reviews for this versatile white wine. For great value in Chardonnay, it can't be beat.
#22654
Original Price: $15.99
Sale Price: $13.59

5. Juliénas, Dom. du Clos du Fief, Michel Tête - 2006 (750 mL)
This red wine is made from the Gamay grape variety. On the nose you'll find hints of black pepper, followed by ample fresh cherry fruit on the palate.
#13055
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

4. Fleurie, Clos de Roilette - 2007 (750 mL)
Complex, structured, broad-shouldered cru Beaujolais with pure berry fruit, floral notes, and herbal accents. Excellent now - and even better in five years.
#28964
Original Price: $21.99
Sale Price: $18.69

3. Beaujolais-Villages "La Lutine", Dom. des Terres Vivantes - 2007 (750 mL)
This wine is an irresistibly lively pink-hued ruby red in the glass, a color that captures the spirit of the wine. On the nose and palate, the wine gives plenty of juicy Gamay fruit along with a healthy dose of granite mineral on the finish. The name "Lutine" means "little elfin girl" - which seems an apt image to associate with this light, joyous wine. The estate farms sustainably with no chemical pesticides, and uses only natural yeasts.
#20993
Original Price: $13.99
Sale Price: $11.89

2. Beaujolais-Villages, La Burgette - 2007 (750 mL)
Sometimes people forget that Beaujolais is actually a part of Burgundy. The wines of Beaujolais are made from Gamay - Burgundy’s “other red grape.” This bottle is full of tangy red fruits and makes a delightful match for sausages or even grilled salmon.
#18416
Original Price: $10.99
Sale Price: $8.99

1. Côte-de-Brouilly "Terres Dorees" Jean-Paul Brun - 2007 (750 mL)
Jean-Paul Brun refuses to use laboratory yeasts or add sugar to his wines - two practices that are very common in Beaujolais. His wines turn out light-bodied, juicy, and eminently quaffable: this one is brimming with red fruit.
#20318
Original Price: $18.99
Sale Price: $16.14
